* Context and Motivation

WP-0003 produced a ~1600-line spirit ecology survey covering 7 regions,
42+ spirit types, a functional mapping for Aburaya, story-compose
extensions, and a digital twin data model. The survey is well-structured
and epistemically honest — it marks its own gaps and rates mapping
confidence. But every claim originates from LLM training knowledge.
Sources are named but not verified: no URLs, no confirmed page numbers,
no cross-checked editions. The acceptance criterion "all claims sourced"
is the one partial.

This is a structural limitation, not a failure of execution. The
executing spirit did the best work possible within its constraints.
But unverified ethnography is not a source of truth — it is a
hypothesis about what the sources say.

** Why a second survey, not a verification pass

A verification pass over WP-0003 would inherit its frame: the same
regional boundaries, the same spirit categories, the same emphasis.
Confirmation bias would make it a rubber stamp. What we want is an
*independent derivation* — a second survey conducted from primary and
secondary sources accessible via web research, producing its own
structure, its own emphases, its own gaps. Where the two surveys agree,
confidence is high. Where they diverge, we have a genuine question
that the gazetteer (WP-0004) must flag for resolution.

Two independent maps of the same territory, overlaid. The
disagreements are more valuable than the agreements.

** What this enables for WP-0004

The gazetteer gains a =confidence= field per entry:

| Situation | Confidence |
|-----------+------------|
| Both surveys agree, with verified source | =confirmed= |
| Both surveys agree, source named but unverified | =convergent= |
| Surveys disagree on detail | =disputed= — note both versions |
| Only WP-0003 covers this (no verified source found) | =unverified= |
| Only WP-0006 covers this (WP-0003 missed it) | =verified-only= |
| Neither survey covers this but fieldwork reports exist | =field-report= |

This confidence taxonomy is the real deliverable. It tells the
story-compose agent: "you can use this vocabulary with confidence"
vs. "this might be wrong — use with caution, flag for human review."

* Specification

** Output: =domains/epistem/surveys/spirit-ecology-verified.org=

An Org-mode survey, same domain as WP-0003's output. The file name
signals its relationship: it is not a replacement but a companion.

** Method

The executing spirit must:

1. *Use web search* for every claim. No reliance on training knowledge
   alone. If a fact cannot be found via web search, it is marked
   =[unverifiable via web]= and excluded from the verified count.

2. *Cite URLs* for every substantive claim. Bibliographic references
   are acceptable only if the source text is available online (Google
   Books preview, JSTOR abstract, university repository, etc.) and
   the URL is provided.

3. *Prefer primary and scholarly sources* over travel blogs, Wikipedia
   summary paragraphs, and tourism websites. Acceptable sources
   include:
   - Published ethnographies (books and journal articles)
   - Museum and university collections with documented provenance
   - Government gazetteers and survey reports
   - Documentary films with scholarly commentary
   - Oral history projects with institutional backing
   - Well-sourced Wikipedia articles (follow the footnotes, cite the
     underlying source, not the Wikipedia page)

4. *Build its own regional structure.* Do not copy WP-0003's outline.
   Let the sources dictate which regions get depth and which remain
   thin. If web-accessible sources cluster around Kullu and Tibet
   (likely — these are the most documented), then Kullu and Tibet
   get depth, and Karakoram stays thin. This is honest.

5. *Note where WP-0003 and this survey diverge.* A final section
   should compare the two: what WP-0003 got right, what it got
   wrong, what it covered that this survey cannot verify, and what
   this survey found that WP-0003 missed.

** Structure

*** Part 1: Regional Spirit Taxonomies (verified)

Same scope as WP-0003 Part 1, but:
- Every spirit type cited with at least one verified source
- Landscape associations grounded in specific documented cases
- Human interfaces described from ethnographic accounts, not inference
- Depth varies by source availability — this is a feature, not a bug

*** Part 2: Cross-Regional Patterns (verified)

Same scope as WP-0003 Part 2, but each pattern supported by at least
two independent sources from different regions. Patterns that cannot
be verified are noted as "plausible but unverified."

*** Part 3: Functional Mapping for Aburaya (verified)

Same functional categories as WP-0003 Part 3. For each mapping:
- The Himalayan source tradition is documented with verified sources
- The "where it breaks" analysis is grounded in specific ethnographic
  observations, not LLM inference
- The vocabulary recommendations carry source provenance

*** Part 4: Comparison with WP-0003

A structured comparison:

| Category | Count |
|----------+-------|
| Claims confirmed by both surveys with source | N |
| Claims in WP-0003 that this survey cannot verify | N |
| Claims in WP-0003 that this survey contradicts | N |
| New material found by this survey, absent from WP-0003 | N |
| Vocabulary recommendations that agree | N |
| Vocabulary recommendations that differ | N |

For each disagreement, a brief note explaining the divergence.

*** Part 5: Source Bibliography (verified)

Every source cited in the survey, with:
- Full bibliographic reference
- URL where the source (or a substantive excerpt) is accessible
- Date accessed
- Brief note on what this source contributes

** What this survey does NOT include

- *Story-compose extensions* — WP-0003's Part 4 is creative
  application, not ethnographic claim. It does not need verification;
  it needs taste. The verified survey does not duplicate it.
- *Digital twin data model* — WP-0003's Part 5 is a technical
  proposal, not an ethnographic claim. It stands on its own merits.
  No duplication here.
- *Completeness* — this survey will be smaller than WP-0003. That is
  the point. Verified knowledge is sparser than inferred knowledge.
  The gap between the two is the measure of our uncertainty.

* Execution

** Spirit

The epistem-guardian, with web-search powers. This WP requires
extensive web research — the spirit must search, read, extract, and
cite. The harness must support web search and web fetch.

** Order

1. Begin with the best-documented regions: Kullu (Chetwode, Rosser,
   Harcourt are likely findable) and Tibet/Bön (Nebesky-Wojkowitz,
   Tucci, Samuel are standard references with online presence).
2. Extend to Ladakh/Zanskar (Crook & Osmaston, Gutschow) and Nepal
   (Maskarinec, Holmberg, Peters).
3. Attempt Kinnaur, Spiti, Karakoram — these may be thin. Document
   what is findable and what is not.
4. Write cross-regional patterns from the verified material.
5. Write functional mapping with source provenance.
6. Compare with WP-0003.

** Constraints

- *Token budget*: this WP will require many web searches. The
  executing agent should batch searches efficiently and not waste
  context on tangential reading.
- *Access limitations*: many ethnographic sources are behind paywalls
  or in physical libraries. The survey should note when a source is
  cited in secondary literature but not directly accessible. This is
  not a failure — it is honest scholarship.
- *Time*: this is a research-heavy WP. It will take longer than
  WP-0003, which could draw freely on training knowledge. Budget
  accordingly.

* Dependencies

- *Depends on*: WP-0001 (epistem domain exists)
- *Informed by*: WP-0003 (but must not copy its structure — the
  independence of the two surveys is the point)
- *Feeds*: WP-0004 (provides the =confidence= field for gazetteer
  entries, alongside WP-0003)

* What This Proves

That the project distinguishes between fluent knowledge and verified
knowledge. That an LLM-generated survey, however well-structured, is
a hypothesis until checked against sources. That two independent
derivations of the same territory, compared honestly, produce
something more trustworthy than either alone.

And practically: that the gazetteer (WP-0004) can carry a =confidence=
field that means something — not just "I think this is right" but
"these two independent surveys agree (or disagree), and here are the
sources."
