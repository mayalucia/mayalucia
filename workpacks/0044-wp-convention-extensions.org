* Genesis

While closing WP-0033 (orient-to-mayalucia power) in a Claude Code
session, mayadev and mu2tau reviewed the open work backlog and began
triaging WP-0034 (capabilities revision). The triage surfaced
WP-0037's architectural findings, which needed to be scoped out of
WP-0034. That scoping discussion produced an insight about the power
inventory's three tiers (organisational, domain, embodiment). The
insight had nowhere to live — it was valuable, but ephemeral in the
conversation. mu2tau asked: "we should have an insight section in
WPs." That led to WP-0043 (org tagging), which introduced
=#+begin_insight= blocks. mu2tau then asked: "shouldn't we also have
a context section describing what led to the WP?" — distinguishing
/genesis/ (the thread of decisions that produced this WP) from
/context/ (the problem space the WP addresses).

This WP formalises both conventions.

* Context

The WP convention (=develop/work-packages.org= v0.5) specifies 5
required sections and 3 optional. Two gaps:

1. *No place for theoretical findings.* Insights emerge during
   execution — connections between the work and broader patterns. They
   currently live in conversation transcripts and agent memory, both
   volatile. The =What This Proves= section captures /what the completed
   work demonstrates/, but not the smaller findings that accumulate
   along the way.

2. *No provenance trail.* The =Context and Motivation= section
   describes the problem space — /why does this work exist/. It does
   not describe /how we got here/ — which conversation, which earlier
   WP, which moment of recognition produced this WP. Genesis is the
   difference between "here is the problem" and "here is how we
   noticed the problem."

* Specification

** 1. =#+begin_insight= / =#+end_insight= blocks

A new optional block type for WPs and convention documents.

*** Purpose

Capture theoretical findings — connections, tensions, patterns —
that emerge during the work. Insights are /what the work taught us/,
not /what the work produced/.

*** Format

#+begin_example
,#+begin_insight
The two-phase design mirrors the sutradhar companion's
perception-cognition loop: Phase A is /attend/, Phase B is
/synthesise/. If run as a substrate experiment, the Phase A → Phase B
transition is where substrate character will show most clearly.
,#+end_insight
#+end_example

*** Placement

Insight blocks appear inline, near the content that prompted them.
They are not gathered into a single section — they are contextual
deposits, like marginalia. A WP may have zero or many insight blocks.

*** Properties

- Org treats unknown =#+begin_/#+end_= pairs as exportable special
  blocks — they render cleanly in HTML export, and can be styled with
  CSS (=div.insight=)
- Greppable: =grep -r "begin_insight" workpacks/= gives every
  theoretical finding in the project
- Machine-readable: a future tool could extract insight blocks into
  an apprentis corpus

*** What an insight block is NOT

- Not a comment (=#= or =;;=) — comments are for the author during
  drafting. Insights are findings that belong in the document.
- Not the =What This Proves= section — that section summarises what
  the /completed/ work demonstrates. Insight blocks capture findings
  /during/ execution, including partial or surprising ones.
- Not a decision record — decisions go in =Settled Questions= or in
  the specification. Insights record /understanding/, not /choice/.

** 2. =* Genesis= section

A new optional section for WPs.

*** Purpose

Record the provenance trail — what conversation, work, or moment of
recognition produced this WP. Genesis answers "how did we get here?"
while Context answers "what is the problem?"

*** Placement

Before =* Context=. The reading order becomes:

#+begin_example
,* Genesis          (optional — how we got here)
,* Context          (required — what the problem is)
,* Inventory        (required — what already exists)
,* Specification    (required — what to build)
...
#+end_example

*** Content

Genesis is narrative, not structured. It names:

- Which WP, session, or conversation surfaced the need
- What the human or spirit noticed
- The chain of reasoning from observation to WP

Genesis sections are typically short (3–10 lines). They are written
once, at drafting time, and not updated. They are the WP's birth
story.

*** When to include

Not every WP needs a genesis. Include one when:

- The WP emerged from a non-obvious connection between earlier work
- The provenance chain crosses multiple WPs or sessions
- Future readers would benefit from understanding /why this WP exists
  now/ rather than earlier or later

Skip it when the WP is a straightforward next step (e.g. "WP-0002
follows WP-0001 because the convention says so").

** 3. Retroactive insight mining

Existing WPs contain insights in prose form — buried in analysis
sections, in =What This Proves=, in inline observations. These should
be retroactively wrapped in =#+begin_insight= blocks where they are
genuine theoretical findings, not just summaries of results.

#+begin_insight
Retroactive insight mining is itself an instance of the tagging task
(WP-0043): reading a document, recognising a pattern, marking it. The
difference is that tags classify /topics/ while insight blocks mark
/understanding/. Both are metadata deposited by a reader into a
document. If the tagging WP runs as a substrate experiment, insight
mining could be a parallel task — same document, different metadata
layer.
#+end_insight
