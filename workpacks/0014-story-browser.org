* Context and Motivation

Twelve stories now inhabit the Western Himalaya — field notes, logbooks,
geological surveys, testimony, academic satire. Each translates a system
concept into the valley's language. Each is pinned to a geography, a
document type, a set of characters, a season.

A linear table of contents — story 1, story 2, story 3 — collapses this
richness into a single dimension. The stories exist in a manifold:

- *Geography*: the Thread Walker moves. Tirthan has five stories; Parvati,
  Baspa, Sutlej have one each. The Karakoram is empty, waiting.
- *Translation*: what system concept each story carries. Identity, mechanism,
  perception, convention, memory — a conceptual graph.
- *Time*: not composition order but story-internal time. Geological strata
  (story #9) reach back millions of years; the dāk runners (#12) are
  colonial. The seasonal cycle clusters monsoon stories together.
- *Document type*: field notes, logbook, geological survey, testimony,
  academic satire. The texture of the form.
- *Voice*: the Thread Walker is continuous. The kohli, the gur, the weaver,
  the grandson — they form their own constellation.

The browser lets the reader choose which projection to walk through.
Click a valley on the map, get stories clustered there. Follow a concept,
see which stories translate it. Follow a character, trace their appearances.

** Why now

The project has a working constellation viewer for its own architecture
(=modules/sutradhar/website/project-constellation/=). Same stack — ClojureScript,
Reagent, D3-force, SVG — can render a story browser. The data exists
(12 stories with structured metadata in =develop/story-development.org=).
The convention exists. What's missing is the navigation experience.

Also: a book is coming. The browser is the book's digital companion —
not a replacement for reading, but an orientation device. The reader who
has finished one story can discover which story to read next by geography,
by concept, by character — not by page number.

* Inventory (What Already Exists)

info panel (right). Force-directed graph of project entities. Data in EDN
(=data.cljs=). Drill-down hierarchy. Zoom/pan. ~1,400 lines of ClojureScript.

- Force simulation (=force.cljs=) — link distances, charge, collision

- Data structure: entities become stories, edges become relationships
- Node rendering: phase crystals → story glyphs (document type icons?)

* Specification

** Dimensions (the browser's axes)

The browser renders stories as nodes in a space. The reader selects
which dimensions define the space. Each dimension is a /projection/:

*** Geography

A map of the Western Himalaya. Stories pinned to their locations. The
Thread Walker's route as a path connecting them. Zoom into Tirthan to
see five stories separated by altitude and gorge. Pan north — the
Karakoram is empty, a frontier.

First mockup: 2D SVG topographic map with elevation contours derived
from DEM data. Later: 3D terrain (WebGPU via mayaportal, or Three.js).

*** Translation (concept graph)

Nodes are system concepts (identity, mechanism, perception, convention,
memory, generation, orchestration). Edges connect concepts that appear
in the same story. Stories are the edges — click an edge to read the
story that bridges two concepts. Or: stories are nodes, concept-similarity
is the edge.

First mockup: force-directed graph, reusing the constellation viewer's
D3 simulation.

*** Time

A timeline — but not linear calendar time. Three temporal registers:

- *Deep time*: geological (story #9 reaches Tethys)
- *Historical*: colonial dāk runners (#12), pre-colonial kuhls (#10)
- *Seasonal*: monsoon / post-monsoon / winter

Stories placed on a spiral or layered timeline.

*** Document type

A gallery of textures. Field notes look like field notes. The geological
survey looks like a survey. The academic satire looks like a journal paper.
Each story represented by its document form, not just its title.

*** Voice (character constellation)

Characters as nodes. Edges connect characters who appear in the same story.
The Thread Walker is central (appears in all). The kohli family spans
stories #10 and #12. The gur appears only in #7.

** Emergent dimensions

The dimensions above are /inputs/ — what we gave the story-teller.
They describe the brief, not the work. The browser must also surface
what /emerged/ from the translation — patterns the LLM engine found
when it moved system concepts into the valley's language. These are
the story-teller's cognitive signature, and they evaluate its capabilities
as surely as any benchmark.

*** Human-landscape entanglement

How does the landscape act /through/ the human? The kohli knows gradient
because his hands have placed stones in water for generations. The dāk
runner knows the trail because his legs have carried the custom. The gur
speaks in the nag devta's voice because the mountain speaks /through/
bodies, not /to/ them.

This dimension tracks: for each story, what is the mode of entanglement
between the human characters and the natural phenomena around them?
Rivers, mountains, rain, snow, glaciers, gorges, hot springs — not
as scenery but as actors that shape the humans who inhabit them.

*** Knowledge modality

Where does knowing live? Each story implicitly asks this question and
arrives at a different answer:

- In the register (written) — story #12
- In the legs (embodied) — story #12
- In the water (material) — stories #7, #10
- In the thread (structural) — stories #1, #11
- In the stone (geological) — story #9
- In the loom (mechanical) — story #11
- In the breath (temporal) — story #7

A dimension that tracks not /what/ is known but /what medium carries
the knowing/. Two stories may translate the same concept but locate
knowledge in different substrates. The browser should make this visible.

*** Loss and persistence

What survives when the practice ends? The deepest recurring tension
across the stories — not put there by the brief, but emerging from the
translation work:

- The gradient persists (physics). The custom does not (enactment).
- The mineral signature persists (chemistry). The spirit's name does not
  (relation).
- The register survives. The trail knowledge does not.
- The channel carries water after the kuhl is abandoned. The runner
  stops when the road arrives.

This is the weft of the weave — the thread that runs /across/ the
stories without being asked for. A dimension that tracks: for each
story, what persists and what is lost when the medium changes?

*** Objects as actors

The loom teaches the weaver's body the pattern. The kuhl's gradient
carries decisions the kohli made. The register preserves what the
custom could not. The settling pool filters what the channel carries.

Objects in these stories have structural agency — not metaphorical,
but functional. The loom is not a prop; it is a character. The
register is not a record; it is the third hand's attempt to make
custom survive.

This dimension asks: /what does the object do that the human cannot?/
It reveals the relationship between instruments, mechanisms, and spirits
— the warp and weft of the weave of existence, as the project's
guiding philosophy would have it.

*** Threshold moments

Where does understanding shift? Almost every story has a liminal
passage — geographical (a pass, a gorge, a descent) and cognitive
(the moment the grandson says "trail knowledge lives in legs, not
in registers"). These thresholds are the story's structural joints.

The browser could mark these moments as inflection points — the place
where the reader's understanding pivots, not just the Thread Walker's
route.

*** Silence and absence

What the story refuses to say. The Thread Walker does not explain what
she does not understand. The Phantom Faculty governs without existing.
The Karakoram is empty on the map. The Hindi retelling translates the
same structure into a different register without explaining why.

Absence is information. A dimension that tracks: what is /not/ in
each story, and what does that omission reveal about the story-teller's
limits — or its discipline?

*** Ecological web — the spirits that never see light

The dimensions above track human-landscape relationships. This dimension
tracks the relationships /between non-human actors/ — the full ecological
web that the stories have only touched in fragments.

The lammergeier (/hadda-tod/, bone-breaker; Jatayu's kin in the Rāmāyaṇa)
circles at 7,500m above the Parvati pyramid, dropping bones onto
ossuaries to shatter them. The Himalayan griffon vulture (/giddh/)
shadows the sheep herds moving to fresh pastures above the tree line.
The ibex walks ridges where juniper grows. The juniper grows where the
monsoon reaches. The monsoon reaches where the orography permits. And
below all of it, tectonic forces — spirits that have never seen the
light of day — push Nanga Parbat 1cm higher each year while the Indus
grinds it down. The mountain grows because it erodes. The erosion drives
the uplift. The tectonic aneurysm is a feedback loop 50 million years
old.

Change the tree line, change the juniper, change where the ibex walks,
change where the lammergeier hunts. The Himalayan brown bear's range
contracts as glaciers retreat. The snow leopard follows the ibex follows
the grass follows the snowmelt follows the glacier follows the climate
follows the monsoon follows the orography follows the tectonic thrust.
Every link is a story. Every broken link is a loss.

The stories have touched this web in fragments:
- The kuhl carries water shaped by gradient shaped by geology (#10)
- The gur speaks for a nag devta who /is/ the river's character (#7)
- The mineral signature persists through geological time (#9)
- The glacier acts — surging, advancing, retreating (#12 survey material)

But no story has yet followed the chain all the way — from tectonic
thrust through altitude zonation through species behaviour through
spirit tradition through human practice and back. The ecological web
is the dimension that connects the mountain's blind upward push to
the lammergeier's circling to the shepherd's seasonal migration to the
bitan's juniper smoke. It is the warp and weft at the largest scale —
the weave of existence itself.

This dimension is not yet fully present in the stories. It is the
frontier the browser should make visible as /absence/ — the connections
the stories haven't made yet, the relationships waiting to be followed.
The Karakoram surveys (WP-0012, WP-0013) have gathered the raw material:
peri at altitude, glacier marriage, the ibex cult across 5,000 years,
the lammergeier's sky burial. The stories that follow will enter
this web.

** Data format

Story metadata in EDN, following the constellation viewer's pattern.
One =data.cljs= (or =data.edn= loaded at runtime) with:

#+begin_example
{:stories [{:id "thread-walkers"
            :number 1
            :title "The Thread Walkers"
            :slug "the-thread-walkers"
            :setting {:region "Lahaul/Spiti" :places ["Kullu-Tibet border"]}
            :document-type :field-notes
            :date "2026-02-23"
            :characters ["thread-walker" "guild-members"]
            :concepts [:identity-registry :guild :relay :disposition :harness]
            :illustrations 4
            :excerpt "..."}
           ...]
 :concepts [{:id :identity-registry
             :name "Spirit identity registry"

             :introduced-in 1}
            ...]
 :characters [{:id "thread-walker"
               :name "The Thread Walker"
               :appears-in [1 2 3 4 5 7 8 9 10 11 12]
               :role "narrator/observer"}
              ...]
 :edges [...]}
#+end_example

** First mockup (Phase 1)

Two dimensions rendered side by side or as switchable views:

1. *Geography view*: 2D SVG map with story pins. Click a pin → info panel
   shows story metadata, illustration thumbnails, translation keys.
   The Tirthan cluster is zoomable.

2. *Concept view*: Force-directed graph of translation keys. Stories as
   edges connecting the concepts they translate. Hover a concept → highlight
   all stories that translate it.

Both views share the same info panel component. Switching dimensions
re-layouts the same story data.

** Where it lives

=website/story-browser/= — sibling to =website/project-constellation/=.
Same shadow-cljs build pattern. Output JS to =website/static/js/story-browser.js=.
Hugo page at =website/content/writing/browser.md= (or similar) mounts the
component.

Shares =deps.edn= dependencies with the constellation viewer. May share
source files (state management, edge rendering) via a shared CLJS namespace
or simple copy-and-adapt.

* Execution Order

1. *Data compilation* — extract story metadata from =develop/story-development.org=
   into EDN. Manual curation, not automated parsing (the data is small and
   benefits from human judgment about what to include).

2. *Geography mockup* — 2D SVG map with story pins. Reuse DEM contour
   data from =domains/parbati/= for the Kullu/Tirthan area. Schematic
   outlines for other regions.

3. *Concept mockup* — force-directed graph of translation keys. Fork from
   constellation viewer, replace entity data with concept data.

4. *Info panel* — story detail view: title, setting, excerpt, illustration
   thumbnails, character list, translation keys, link to full story.

5. *View switching* — toggle between geography and concept projections.
   Same data, different spatial layout.

6. *Integration* — Hugo page, build pipeline, deploy.

7. *Human review* — does the navigation feel right? Does the reader
   discover stories they want to read?

Phases 2–3 can be parallelised. Phase 4 serves both views.

* What This Proves

Three things, at increasing depth:

1. *The constellation viewer generalises.* The same ClojureScript +
   D3-force + Reagent stack that renders the project's own structure can
   render the project's creative output. The viewer is a genus, not a
   species.

2. *The stories carry more structure than we gave them.* The input
   dimensions (geography, concepts, document type) are the brief. The
   emergent dimensions (entanglement, knowledge modality, loss, object
   agency, thresholds, silence) are what the story-teller /found/. The
   browser makes both visible — and the gap between them is the
   story-teller's cognitive contribution.

3. *The browser is a visual evaluation of the story-teller spirit.*
   Each emergent dimension is a capability the LLM engine demonstrated
   through creative work, not through a benchmark. Human-landscape
   entanglement is not a prompt instruction — it is a pattern the engine
   discovered when translating system concepts into valley language. The
   browser lets us see /what the engine sees/ when it does this work.
   This is the Feynman imperative applied to creative AI: we understand
   the story-teller's cognition by building the instrument that renders
   its emergent structure.

* Open Questions

- *3D terrain*: the first mockup uses 2D SVG. When does 3D terrain
  arrive? mayaportal has WebGPU rendering; parbati has DEM data. But
  wiring them into a browser component is a separate WP. The 2D map
  should be designed so 3D is an upgrade, not a rewrite.

- *Executor*: who builds this? It is ClojureScript — the sutradhar's
  language. But it is reader-facing, not project-facing. A new spirit?
  Or sutradhar-guardian extending its scope? Or mayadev as coordinator
  with sutradhar-guardian executing?

- *The Hindi story* (10h): does it appear as a separate pin in the
  geography view (same location, different document) or as a variant
  within story #10's info panel?

- *Story #6 (Phantom Faculty)*: no geography. Does it float in the map
  view? Appear only in concept view? Get a special "abstract" region?

- *Interactive reading*: does clicking "read" in the info panel navigate
  to the Hugo story page, or does the browser itself become a reading
  surface?

- *Sound*: should the geography view have ambient sound? Wind at altitude,
  water in the gorge, loom shuttle in Gushaini? This is an experience
  design question, not a code question.

- *The book*: if this browser is the book's digital companion, what is
  the book's physical form? Print? PDF? Illustrated hardcover? The
  browser and the book are two projections of the same manifold — they
  should be designed together.
