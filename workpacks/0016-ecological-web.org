* Context and Motivation

The story browser (WP-0014, DP-0003) includes an emergent dimension
called "ecological web" — the relationships between non-human actors
across the Western Himalaya. This dimension connects tectonic forces
to altitude zonation to species behaviour to spirit traditions to
human practice.

The Diamer surveys (WP-0012, WP-0013) gathered some of this material
for the Karakoram. This WP extends the ecological web to the full
story geography: Tirthan, Parvati, Baspa, Spiti, Lahaul, Sutlej —
the monsoon valleys where the stories live.

This is homework — not a story, not a browser component. Raw material
for the browser's ecological web dimension and for future stories.

** Why this matters

The stories touch the ecological web in fragments: the kuhl carries
water shaped by geology (#10), the gur speaks for a nag devta who /is/
the river (#7), the mineral signature persists through geological
time (#9). But no story — and no survey — has mapped the full chain
for the monsoon valleys. The Diamer surveys did this for the Karakoram
(tectonic aneurysm → glacier → species → spirit). The monsoon valleys
deserve the same depth.

** Great Himalayan National Park

The GHNP covers the upper catchments of the Tirthan, Sainj, Parvati,
and Jiwa rivers. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Biodiversity
data exists but has not been surveyed for this project.

* Specification

** Part A: Altitude zonation chains — monsoon valleys

For each story region (Tirthan, Parvati, Baspa, Spiti/Lahaul, Sutlej),
document the ecological chain:

1. *Geology*: dominant rock type, tectonic context, erosion rate
2. *Altitude zones*: subtropical → temperate → subalpine → alpine → nival.
   What grows and lives at each zone? How do zones shift with aspect
   (south-facing vs north-facing)?
3. *Keystone species*: which species define each zone? Deodar below,
   birch/juniper above, alpine meadow, rock/ice.
4. *Raptors*: lammergeier, Himalayan griffon, golden eagle. Local names
   (Pahari, Shina, Hindi). Hunting ranges. Relationship to herding
   (griffon follows sheep to alpine pastures). Mythology (Jatayu,
   Dakini sky burial, local legends).
5. *The monsoon boundary*: where does monsoon influence end? How does
   this shift the zones compared to the Karakoram?

** Part B: Species–spirit connections

For each story region, document connections between:

- *Nag devtas and rivers*: which rivers have nag devta associations?
  What does the devta "know" about the river's character?
- *Ibex/markhor and petroglyphs*: the ibex cult extends from Diamer
  into the monsoon valleys? Or is it Karakoram-specific?
- *Bears, leopards, and altitude*: Himalayan brown bear range changes.
  Snow leopard range in GHNP area. Local names and legends.
- *Trees as spirits*: deodar as sacred (devdār = "timber of the gods").
  Juniper as sacred across Dardic world. Any sacred trees in the
  Tirthan/Parvati stories?

** Part C: Shifting baselines

What has changed in living memory?

- Tree line shifts (climate change, logging, grazing pressure)
- Species range changes (brown bear sightings, snow leopard camera traps)
- River character changes (damming, hydropower, glacial retreat)
- Pastoral migration patterns (sheep to alpine meadows — still happening?
  Changing routes? Changing seasons?)

This section provides the "loss" data for the browser's ecological
web dimension — broken links in the chain.
