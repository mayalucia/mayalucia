* Specification

** Part A: Deep Time — Before Humans

Search for what is known about the Nanga Parbat region across
geological and evolutionary time. For each epoch, seek: what the
landscape looked like, what lived there, what evidence exists, and
what academic sources document it.

*** A.1 Tethys to Collision (~250–50 Ma)

- The Tethys Sea: what did the ocean look like where the Karakoram
  now stands? Marine fossils in the Karakoram and western Himalaya.
- Gondwana breakup: India's northward drift. Speed, trajectory.
- The Kohistan island arc: oceanic crust caught in the collision.
  What was it before it became the suture zone?

*** A.2 Collision to Uplift (~50–5 Ma)

- Early Himalayan orogeny: when did the mountains first emerge?
- The proto-Indus: the river predates the mountain — what was its
  course before the syntaxis began?
- Climate: the development of the monsoon system. When did the rain
  shadow form?
- Flora and fauna: what grew on early Himalayan slopes?

*** A.3 Pleistocene (2.6 Ma – 12 ka)

- Glacial cycles: how far did glaciers extend? Ice age Nanga Parbat.
- *Megafauna*: what walked these valleys? Stegodon, woolly rhinoceros,
  cave hyena, snow leopard ancestors? What fossil evidence exists
  from the western Himalaya and Karakoram?
- The decompression melting pulse (1 Ma): the mountain was actively
  producing new granite while megafauna walked below.
- Palaeolithic humans: any evidence of pre-modern human presence in
  the Diamer/Gilgit region? Stone tools, rock shelters?

*** A.4 Holocene Deep History (12 ka – 3 ka)

- The earliest rock carvings at Thalpan and Chilas: 8,000 BCE.
  What do the oldest carvings depict? Animals? Symbols? Hunting scenes?
- Neolithic transition in the region: when did agriculture arrive?
- Trade routes: the Karakoram as corridor between Central Asia,
  China, and the subcontinent.
- Climate oscillations: the Holocene optimum, the 4.2 ka event —
  how did they affect the region?

** Part B: Deep Human History — The First Stories

The v0 covers the petroglyphs in the context of the Diamer-Basha Dam
threat and gives a factual overview of the bitan tradition. This part
goes deeper: the petroglyphs as a *primary source* for understanding
who lived here across millennia, and the pre-Islamic religion as a
*coherent system* rather than scattered ethnographic details.

*** B.1 Petroglyphs as Primary Source

v0 baseline: Jettmar's mission, 30,000 carvings, 10 writing systems,
the Altar Rock. *Go deeper on*:

- Detailed catalogue of *motifs*: hunting scenes, animals (ibex,
  markhor, snow leopard), ritual figures, Buddhist imagery. What is
  the relative chronology? Which motifs are oldest?
- What each *writing system* tells us about who passed through and
  when — Brahmi, Sogdian, Middle Persian, Chinese, Tibetan, Hebrew,
  Kharoshthi. Trade routes implied by linguistic diversity.
- *Pre-Buddhist motifs*: Jettmar linked maskoid motifs near Chilas to
  the Early Bronze Age Okunev Culture in Siberia. What does this
  Siberian connection imply? Sources on the Okunev link specifically.
- The Altar Rock (Thalpan): Jataka scenes, Buddha's temptation,
  the sermon at Benares. Seek *image sources* (archaeological
  publications, museum archives).

*** B.2 Pre-Islamic Religion of the Dards

- Jettmar's /Religions of the Hindukush/ (1975/2023): the Kafir
  religion with Vedic god-names, Kalasha cosmology, Shina pre-Islamic
  practices.
- The relationship between Dardic religion and Vedic religion —
  shared god-names, divergent practices.
- The ibex cult: ibex horns on graves, ibex petroglyphs, ibex as
  sacred animal. Any connection to the markhor (screw-horned goat)?
- Juniper as sacred: cedar/juniper worship across the Dardic world.
  The bitan's juniper smoke as residue of this older practice.

*** B.3 Oral Tradition and Song

- Burushaski oral literature: folktales, fairy/peri stories.
  Hermann Berger's collections (1998).
- Shina oral tradition: songs, proverbs, the twelve musical tunes.
- Kalasha religious songs and festival cycles (Loude & Lièvre).
- Any recorded/transcribed oral histories from Diamer district?

** Part C: Flora, Fauna, and Landscape Entities

v0 has a thin ecology paragraph (A.6). This part gives each organism
its due — not as a species list but as entities with cultural
resonance and story potential. For each species: range/behaviour near
Nanga Parbat, cultural significance (petroglyphs, spirit traditions,
local names), and what makes it visually or narratively interesting.

*** C.1 Mammals — the ones carved on the rocks

- *Himalayan ibex*: the ibex cult — horns on graves, ibex petroglyphs
  (the most frequent animal motif at Thalpan). Current population
  and range. Why this animal, above all others, was sacred.
- *Markhor* (screw-horned goat): Pakistan's national animal. The name
  means "snake-eater" — is there a connection to the nāg/klu
  tradition? Distribution around Nanga Parbat. The spiral horns as
  visual motif.
- *Snow leopard*: range, behaviour, camera trap studies in the
  Diamer/Nanga Parbat area. Cultural significance — ghost of the
  mountain. Any petroglyphic representation?
- *Himalayan brown bear*: any presence in Diamer? The Deosai
  population is nearby.
- *Marmot*: high-altitude ecology, behaviour, sound (the alarm
  whistle that carries across the jūt).

*** C.2 Birds — the ones that fly above the face

- *Bearded vulture* (lammergeier): bone-eater, 3m wingspan, drops
  bones from height to crack them. Range around Nanga Parbat. Any
  local names or spirit associations?
- *Golden eagle, Himalayan griffon*: presence, behaviour.
- *Chukar partridge*: the common bird of the arid valleys. Sound.
- Any species endemic to or notably concentrated in the Diamer area?

*** C.3 Flora — what grows without monsoon

- *Juniper*: species present (Juniperus excelsa, J. macropoda?),
  distribution, ecology. Sacred use — the bitan's smoke. The oldest
  living junipers in the region?
- *Birch* forests at altitude: species, treeline elevation.
- *Alpine meadow* composition (the /jūt/) — what flowers grow there?
  The meadow is the peri's domain; what does it actually look like
  in bloom?
- *Apricot and cherry* orchards in the valleys — the blossoming
  season when peri descend to choose shamans. Varieties, cultivation
  history.
- Any notable medicinal plants used by the bitan?

*** C.4 Rivers and Water

v0 covers the Indus gorge geology and hot springs well. Here, focus on:

- Tributaries and meltwater streams: names, character.
- The hot springs as *ecological niche* — thermophilic organisms?
  Any studies of the microbial ecology at Tato or Tattapani?
- Glacial meltwater chemistry — how does it differ from monsoon-fed
  rivers? What does the water carry down from the mountain?

** Part D: Visual and Sound References

Concrete source material that future stories, illustrations, and
interactive work can draw on. This is research (find existing
material, document URLs), not creative direction.

*** D.1 Visual References

- Photographs of Thalpan petroglyphs — high-quality, from
  archaeological publications (Jettmar's volumes, ISIAO archives,
  Pak-German mission reports). Document URLs or publication references.
- Historical expedition photographs: 1930s German expeditions,
  Buhl's 1953 photos. Where are these archived?
- Geological cross-sections and diagrams of the syntaxis —
  academic papers with reusable figures.
- Satellite imagery of the Indus gorge and glacier systems —
  Landsat, Sentinel, or similar. Accessible sources.
- Salman Rashid's photographs (if published online).

*** D.2 Sound

- Recordings of the twelve Shina musical tunes (Dadang, Daamal,
  Surnai). Any available online? YouTube, SoundCloud, academic
  ethnomusicology archives?
- Any field recordings from the Diamer/Gilgit region — river,
  glacier, wind at altitude?
- Ethnomusicological publications on Shina or Burushaski music
  that include transcriptions or audio references.

* Open Questions

- What megafauna evidence exists for the western Himalaya specifically?
  The Siwalik Hills (south of here) have rich fossil beds, but
  Diamer/Nanga Parbat? The rapid uplift and erosion may have destroyed
  fossil-bearing strata. Be honest if the evidence is thin.
- Are there any sound recordings of Shina shamanic music available
  online? The twelve named tunes are documented; are they listenable?
- How deep is the archaeological record in Diamer before the
  petroglyphs? Stone tools? Rock shelters? The first carvings at
  Thalpan (8,000 BCE) imply human presence earlier — but how much
  earlier?
