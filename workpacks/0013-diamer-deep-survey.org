* Context and Motivation

WP-0012 produced a first-version regional survey of Diamer and the
Nanga Parbat massif — geology, glaciology, spirit traditions,
aesthetics. That survey covers the present and recent past (human
settlement, colonial expeditions, contemporary life). It is homework
for story composition.

This WP goes deeper and wider. The goal is to collect content from the
web that can:
- develop into a *story* (found-document narrative in the MāyāLucIA voice)
- inspire an *illustration* (graphical, animated, or interactive)
- appear as an *entity* in the story world (a spirit, a beast, a
  geological formation, a sound)
- become a *concept* for artistic development (a portal, an experience,
  a guided journey)

The method must be *observable and reproducible* — a WP that any spirit
can execute, not an informal sprint with subagents. The survey v0
showed what a quick pass finds; this WP asks for the depth and breadth
that careful, delegated research provides.

** The vision

An agentic portal for Diamer — a storytelling guide that leads the
explorer through landscape, time, and spirit traditions. Not a
tourist app but a Thread Walker's companion: /"This is Tato — warm,
because the mountain breathes here."/ The portal requires rich,
verified content across deep time.

* Specification

** Part A: Deep Time — Before Humans

Search for what is known about the Nanga Parbat region across
geological and evolutionary time. For each epoch, seek: what the
landscape looked like, what lived there, what evidence exists, and
what academic sources document it.

*** A.1 Tethys to Collision (~250–50 Ma)

- The Tethys Sea: what did the ocean look like where the Karakoram
  now stands? Marine fossils in the Karakoram and western Himalaya.
- Gondwana breakup: India's northward drift. Speed, trajectory.
- The Kohistan island arc: oceanic crust caught in the collision.
  What was it before it became the suture zone?

*** A.2 Collision to Uplift (~50–5 Ma)

- Early Himalayan orogeny: when did the mountains first emerge?
- The proto-Indus: the river predates the mountain — what was its
  course before the syntaxis began?
- Climate: the development of the monsoon system. When did the rain
  shadow form?
- Flora and fauna: what grew on early Himalayan slopes?

*** A.3 Pleistocene (2.6 Ma – 12 ka)

- Glacial cycles: how far did glaciers extend? Ice age Nanga Parbat.
- *Megafauna*: what walked these valleys? Stegodon, woolly rhinoceros,
  cave hyena, snow leopard ancestors? What fossil evidence exists
  from the western Himalaya and Karakoram?
- The decompression melting pulse (1 Ma): the mountain was actively
  producing new granite while megafauna walked below.
- Palaeolithic humans: any evidence of pre-modern human presence in
  the Diamer/Gilgit region? Stone tools, rock shelters?

*** A.4 Holocene Deep History (12 ka – 3 ka)

- The earliest rock carvings at Thalpan and Chilas: 8,000 BCE.
  What do the oldest carvings depict? Animals? Symbols? Hunting scenes?
- Neolithic transition in the region: when did agriculture arrive?
- Trade routes: the Karakoram as corridor between Central Asia,
  China, and the subcontinent.
- Climate oscillations: the Holocene optimum, the 4.2 ka event —
  how did they affect the region?

** Part B: Deep Human History — The First Stories

*** B.1 Petroglyphs as Primary Source

- Jettmar and Hauptmann's Pak-German Archaeological Mission:
  30,000 petroglyphs, 5,000 inscriptions on the Indus. Detailed
  catalogue of motifs: hunting scenes, animals (ibex, markhor, snow
  leopard), ritual figures, Buddhist imagery.
- The 10 writing systems: Brahmi, Sogdian, Middle Persian, Chinese,
  Tibetan, Hebrew, Kharoshthi, and others. What do each tell us
  about who passed through?
- *Pre-Buddhist motifs*: Jettmar linked maskoid motifs near Chilas to
  the Early Bronze Age Okunev Culture in Siberia. What does this
  Siberian connection imply?
- The Altar Rock (Thalpan): Jataka scenes, Buddha's temptation,
  the sermon at Benares. Detailed descriptions and photographs.

*** B.2 Pre-Islamic Religion of the Dards

- Jettmar's /Religions of the Hindukush/ (1975/2023): the Kafir
  religion with Vedic god-names, Kalasha cosmology, Shina pre-Islamic
  practices.
- The relationship between Dardic religion and Vedic religion —
  shared god-names, divergent practices.
- The ibex cult: ibex horns on graves, ibex petroglyphs, ibex as
  sacred animal. Any connection to the markhor (screw-horned goat)?
- Juniper as sacred: cedar/juniper worship across the Dardic world.
  The bitan's juniper smoke as residue of this older practice.

*** B.3 Oral Tradition and Song

- Burushaski oral literature: folktales, fairy/peri stories.
  Hermann Berger's collections (1998).
- Shina oral tradition: songs, proverbs, the twelve musical tunes.
- Kalasha religious songs and festival cycles (Loude & Lièvre).
- Any recorded/transcribed oral histories from Diamer district?

** Part C: Flora, Fauna, and Landscape Entities

Search for what lives in and around the Nanga Parbat massif — not
just species lists but the relationship between organisms and the
landscape. For stories and illustrations.

*** C.1 Mammals

- Snow leopard: range, behaviour in the Nanga Parbat area. Sightings,
  camera trap studies. Cultural significance.
- Markhor (screw-horned goat): the national animal of Pakistan.
  Distribution around Nanga Parbat. The name means "snake-eater" —
  is there a connection to the nāg/klu tradition?
- Himalayan ibex: the ibex cult in petroglyphs. Current population.
- Himalayan brown bear: any presence in Diamer?
- Marmot, pika: high-altitude ecology.

*** C.2 Birds

- Bearded vulture (lammergeier): bone-eater, one of the most
  dramatic raptors. Range around Nanga Parbat.
- Golden eagle, Himalayan griffon.
- Any endemic or notable species?

*** C.3 Flora

- Juniper: species, distribution, ecology. Sacred use.
- Birch forests at altitude.
- Alpine meadow composition (the /jūt/).
- Apricot and cherry orchards in the valleys — the blossoming
  season when peri descend.

*** C.4 Rivers and Water

- The Indus as it passes Nanga Parbat: character, colour, sound.
- Tributaries and meltwater streams.
- The hot springs as ecological niche — thermophilic organisms?
- Glacial meltwater chemistry.

* Open Questions

- What megafauna evidence exists for the western Himalaya specifically?
  The Siwalik Hills (south of here) have rich fossil beds, but
  Diamer/Nanga Parbat? The rapid uplift and erosion may have destroyed
  fossil-bearing strata.
- Are there any sound recordings of Shina shamanic music available
  online? The twelve named tunes are documented; are they listenable?
- How deep is the archaeological record in Diamer before the
  petroglyphs? Stone tools? Rock shelters? The first carvings at
  Thalpan (8,000 BCE) imply human presence earlier — but how much
  earlier?
- The portal concept: is this a website (like CruVin), a mobile
  experience, a VR walk, or something else? The medium shapes the
  content requirements.
