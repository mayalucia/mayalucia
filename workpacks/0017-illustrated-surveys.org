* Context and Motivation

WP-0012 and WP-0013 produced ~3,550 lines of Diamer/Nanga Parbat
survey with 206+ URLs across two files. The homework is done. This
WP turns that research into illustrated artifacts across four output
modes:

1. *Inline illustrations* — diagrams alongside survey org text
2. *Visual atlas* — standalone HTML with spatial navigation
3. *Interactive walkthrough* — scroll-driven deep-time narrative
4. *Story illustration kit* — accurate visual elements for future
   Karakoram stories

All four share the same source material and visual language
(matplotlib, parchment/walnut ink aesthetic). The difference is
output format and interactivity.

** Design decisions

- *Matplotlib only* — no AI image generation. The diagrammatic
  register (petroglyph-style line art, geological cross-sections,
  schematic maps) is the project's visual identity.
- *Illustration manifest* as shared layer — a YAML storyboard listing
  every illustration point, consumed by all four output modes.
- *Interactive walkthrough* as self-contained HTML — following the
  Bloch sphere demo pattern (no framework, no build step).
- *Shared helpers module* (=survey_helpers.py=) — reusable matplotlib
  primitives extending the patterns from story illustrations.

* Specification

** Part 1: Complete the illustration set

Extend =generate_illustrations.py= to produce all 18 illustrations
listed in =manifest.yaml=. Each illustration covers one of 9 visual
types: cross-section, timeline, species-plate, motif-catalogue,
map-schematic, elevation-profile, contrast-table, process-diagram,
writing-specimens.

** Part 2: Visual atlas (=atlas.html=)

Standalone HTML page at
=domains/epistem/surveys/illustrated/diamer/atlas.html=:

- Grid layout of all illustration cards
- Navigation by visual type and survey section
- Click to enlarge any illustration
- Serif typography, parchment palette, no UI chrome
- Self-contained (inline CSS, images as data URIs or relative paths)
- Responsive but desktop-first

** Part 3: Interactive deep-time walkthrough

Self-contained HTML at
=domains/epistem/surveys/illustrated/diamer/deep-time/index.html=:

- Scroll-driven narrative from Tethys (250 Ma) to present
- At least 5 illustrated stops (Tethys, collision, orogeny,
  Pleistocene, petroglyphs)
- Pre-rendered matplotlib PNGs for detailed diagrams
- Optional JS canvas for animated transitions
- Intersection Observer for scroll-triggered reveals
- Survey text excerpts as narrative between illustrations
- No framework, no build step

** Part 4: Story illustration kit

The species plates, landscape elements, and petroglyph motifs
produced for the atlas are also the story kit. No separate
deliverable — the same PNGs at
=domains/epistem/surveys/illustrated/diamer/images/= are available
for future story =generate_images.py= scripts to reference or import.

Document the kit in the manifest: which illustrations are suitable
for story use, what palette to match, what scale conventions to follow.

* What This Proves

That the =survey \to illustration= pipeline works as a convention-specified
procedure. That a dense research survey can be made visual without
AI image generation — matplotlib and the petroglyph aesthetic are
sufficient. That the same illustrations serve inline documentation,
spatial navigation, temporal narrative, and story composition.
