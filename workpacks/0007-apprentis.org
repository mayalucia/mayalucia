* Context and Motivation

In March 2026 we installed four agent-shell extensions into our Emacs
environment: =agent-shell-manager= (dashboard), =agent-shell-attention=
(mode-line alerts), =agent-shell-sidebar= (persistent panel), and
=meta-agent-shell= (multi-agent coordination). The last of these
ships CLI tools for inter-agent communication --- =agent-shell-send=,
=agent-shell-ask=, =agent-shell-search= --- that fill a gap our sūtra
protocol does not cover: real-time, in-session, point-to-point
messaging between live spirits.

This surfaced a question that had been latent since the orchestration
gap was first named: *what is the science of how different kinds of
minds develop shared understanding?*

The phantom faculty (=modules/mayapramana=) answered this for teaching
--- five epistemological modes mediating between a curriculum and a
learner. But its v2 document dissolved the hierarchy: "everyone is a
student again." The modes are not teacher→student; they are modes of
/joint inquiry/ between collaborators with different partial
expertise. The question is no longer /how do we teach/ but *how do
collaborators with different partial expertise develop shared
understanding and individual voice simultaneously*.

This is an empirical question. It cannot be answered by design alone.
It requires observation of actual collaboration --- between human and
agent, between agent and agent, across session boundaries and within
them. The meta-agent-shell CLI tools give us instrumentation we did
not have before: logged, timestamped, searchable records of
inter-agent communication.

This motivates:
- A *domain* for the inquiry into collaborative learning between
  different kinds of intelligence
- A *guild* for the spirits who study how understanding forms
- An *empirical study* using meta-agent-shell and sūtra side by side

The domain is not pedagogy. It is not EdTech. It is the post-2025
science of how understanding forms when carbon and silicon collaborate
--- and what that tells us about understanding itself.

** Why "apprentis"

/Apprentis/ --- the apprentice's guild. Where no one graduates because
the substrate keeps shifting. Not /enseignants/ (teachers), not
/étudiants/ (students). Apprentices: collaborators in the act of
learning, permanently. The word carries the right asymmetry ---
apprentices /have/ masters, but the mastery rotates. Today the
physicist is the master and the agent is the apprentice in
thermodynamics; tomorrow the agent is the master and the physicist is
the apprentice in FPGA timing closure. The phantom faculty formalised
this rotation as five modes. Apprentis is the guild that studies the
rotation itself.

** What distinguishes this from epistem

Epistem asks: /how do you organise knowledge so an agent can use it?/
Apprentis asks: /how does understanding form when two different kinds
of intelligence collaborate?/

Epistem is about structure (schemas, collections, query). Apprentis is
about process (how comprehension emerges, what modes of engagement
produce it, where communication breaks down, what the sūtra captures
that in-session messaging misses and vice versa).

Epistem's collections are /what/ is known. Apprentis studies /how/
knowing happens.

A new domain repository: =mayalucia/apprentis=.

The domain of inquiry: the science of collaborative cognition between

human-agent and agent-agent collaboration. What modes of engagement

** Part 3: Commission the domain guardian

This WP does *not* commission a guardian spirit at this time.

The conventional pattern (WP-0001) commissions a guardian at domain
creation. But apprentis is different: its subject matter is the
process of collaboration itself. The guardian's archetype, disposition,
and powers should emerge from the empirical study — from watching
how spirits actually collaborate — not be specified in advance. The
guild starts with zero spirits and grows its first guardian from
observation.

This is deliberate. The guild that studies how understanding forms
should not pretend to understand its own formation before it has
observed anything.

** Part 4: The channel study (first empirical work)

The channel study is the guild's first act: use meta-agent-shell
and sūtra side by side during actual project work, and observe what
flows through each channel.

*** Protocol

During regular project sessions (WP-0002, WP-0003, WP-0004 execution):

1. When two spirits are live in agent-shell buffers and need to
   coordinate, use =agent-shell-send= or =agent-shell-ask= for
   in-session communication
2. For cross-session communication, continue using sūtra relay
3. Record observations in =domains/apprentis/observations/channel-study/=
   as Org files: what was communicated, which channel, why that
   channel felt right, what would have been lost in the other channel

*** Questions to answer

- What kinds of messages naturally flow through the synchronous
  channel? Through the async channel?
- Does the synchronous channel enable coordination that sūtra cannot?
  (e.g. real-time negotiation during coalition work)
- Does the async channel preserve context that in-session messaging
  loses? (e.g. decisions that matter next week, not next minute)
- Should sūtra grow a "fast path" for live spirits, or should the
  two channels remain separate?
- What do the JSONL logs in =~/.meta-agent-shell/logs/= reveal about
  patterns of inter-agent communication?

*** Deliverable

=domains/apprentis/observations/channel-study/report.org= — a
structured observation report with evidence from actual use. Not
theory, not design — empirical observation.

* What This Proves

That the project can study its own process of learning — not as
navel-gazing but as science. That the phantom faculty's five modes are
not just a curriculum design but an empirical hypothesis about how
understanding forms. That the distinction between synchronous and
asynchronous communication channels is not engineering preference but
epistemological choice — different channels produce different kinds of
shared understanding.

And that a guild can begin with zero spirits, because the first act
of understanding is observation, and the human is the first observer.

* Open Questions

- *Guild membership of mayapramana*: this WP assigns mayapramana to
  the apprentis guild. But mayapramana is also a quantum sensing
  project with engineering content that might belong to a future
  instruments guild. For now, the phantom faculty is the dominant
  identity — the five modes are mayapramana's most original
  contribution. Revisit if an instruments guild forms.

- *Relationship to mayadevgeni*: the collaborative-intelligence
  framework predates apprentis. Apprentis is narrower (focused on
  learning specifically) but deeper (empirical, not just philosophical).
  Should mayadevgeni's position documents migrate to apprentis/theory/?

- *The guardian question*: this WP deliberately does not commission a
  guardian. When should one be commissioned? After the channel study
  produces its first report? After a pattern emerges that needs a
  spirit to hold it? The guild's formation is itself an experiment
  in how organisational structure should emerge from observation.

- *Shu-ha-ri in the guild*: the phantom faculty document describes
  shu-ha-ri progression (learn forms → break forms → leave forms).
  Does the guild itself go through shu-ha-ri? Is the channel study
  the shu phase — learning the forms of inter-agent communication?
