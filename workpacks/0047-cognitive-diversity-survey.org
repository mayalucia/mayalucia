* Genesis

While completing WP-0037 Phase 2 analysis (13 substrates tested with
the =author-wp= power), the data revealed patterns too rich for a
project-internal document: tool-foraging fingerprints, convergence
boundaries, the survey-specification trade-off, WP-refusal as a
lineage-specific behaviour. mu2tau proposed publishing the findings as
a research note on mayalucia.dev — but with a thorough, literature-
grounded introduction that frames the problem independently of
MāyāLucIA, then presents MāyāLucIA as a case study. Given the pace
of the field, the survey should be a living document, updated
periodically as new substrates are tested and new literature appears.

This WP produces the initial survey and the infrastructure for
maintaining it. The recurring update cadence is a convention, not a
WP — once this WP completes, updates are maintenance work.

* Context and Motivation

The substrate diversity experiments (WP-0030 through WP-0037) produced
a data set that has no direct analogue in the published literature:

- *13 substrates*, same task, same tools, same evaluation criteria
- *Two experimental phases*: orientation audit (WP-0030/0031) and
  specification authoring (WP-0037)
- *Tool-use telemetry*: exact counts per tool type (Glob, Read, Grep,
  TodoWrite), per substrate
- *Qualitative analysis*: what each substrate saw, missed, refused,
  invented

No existing study combines these five dimensions in a single
experimental frame:

1. *Behavioural fingerprinting* — how different substrates approach
   the same task
2. *Tool-use strategy* — foraging patterns as cognitive signatures
3. *Substrate diversity* — mixing lineages for complementary coverage
4. *Beyond-accuracy evaluation* — convention adherence, interpretive
   divergence, reflection quality
5. *Convention adherence* — whether agents follow organisational
   norms they discover (not just instructions they're given)

The research note should be useful to anyone building multi-agent
systems, selecting substrates for specific tasks, or studying LLM
behavioural diversity. MāyāLucIA provides the case study, not the
framing.

** Two deliverables

1. *Research note* — published on mayalucia.dev under =/papers/=.
   Follows existing paper conventions (TOML front matter, Hugo
   Markdown, "A Human-Machine Collaboration" attribution). Structured
   as: problem introduction (no MāyāLucIA), literature survey, case
   study (MāyāLucIA context), methods, results, conclusions,
   discussion, future directions.

2. *Story* — Thread Walker narrative translating the experimental
   findings. Composed via =story-compose= power from sutradhar.
   Separate WP if the story scope exceeds what can be done inline.

This WP covers deliverable 1. Deliverable 2 is deferred to a follow-up
WP if needed.

** Literature landscape (initial scan, 2026-03-14)

*** Behavioural fingerprinting

- Pei et al. 2025 — Agent behavioural fingerprinting (arXiv 2509.04504)
- Jiang et al. 2025 — Nature Machine Intelligence, LLM personalities
- Munker 2025 — EMNLP, personality profiles in LLMs
- PMC survey — Personality measurement of LLMs

*** Tool-use

- Qu et al. 2025 — LLM Tool Learning Survey (Springer)
- Ding et al. 2025 — MCP-Bench (arXiv 2508.20453)
- Qin et al. 2024 — ToolBench (ICLR 2024)
- BFCL V4 — Berkeley Function Calling Leaderboard

*** Multi-agent diversity

- X-MAS (arXiv 2505.16997) — 47% improvement from mixing architectures
- Intrinsic Memory Agents (arXiv 2508.08997) — Memory as substrate trait
- MultiAgentBench (ACL 2025) — Multi-agent evaluation
- (Check: Talebirad & Nadiri 2025, multi-agent collaboration survey)

*** Evaluation beyond accuracy

- SWE-Bench Pro (arXiv 2509.16941) — Beyond pass/fail
- GAIA / GAIA2 — General AI assistants benchmark
- Agent evaluation survey (arXiv 2507.21504)
- AgentArch (arXiv 2509.10769) — Architecture search for agents

*** Convention adherence / instruction following

- Tripathi 2025 — The Instruction Gap (arXiv 2601.03269, 13 LLMs)
- AGENTIF (NeurIPS 2025) — Agent instruction following
- FireBench (arXiv 2603.04857, March 2026) — Latest benchmark
- IFEval family — Instruction following evaluation

** What the literature lacks

No existing study:

- Tests 10+ substrates on the /same/ task with /same/ tools
- Treats tool-call patterns as cognitive fingerprints (not just success rates)
- Measures convention adherence (not instruction compliance)
- Maps convergence/divergence boundaries across substrates
- Combines all five dimensions in a single experimental frame

This is the gap the research note fills.

*** Outline

1. *Abstract* — 200-word summary
2. *Introduction* — The problem of substrate selection in agentic systems.
   Why accuracy-only evaluation is insufficient. No MāyāLucIA references.
   Frame around: monoculture risk, tool-use as cognitive signature,
   convention adherence as a missing evaluation axis.
3. *Related Work* — Structured by the five research threads:
   a. Behavioural fingerprinting (Pei, Jiang, Munker)
   b. Tool-use benchmarking (Springer survey, MCP-Bench, ToolBench, BFCL)
   c. Multi-agent diversity (X-MAS, memory agents, MultiAgentBench)
   d. Beyond-accuracy evaluation (SWE-Bench Pro, GAIA, AgentArch)
   e. Convention adherence (Instruction Gap, AGENTIF, FireBench, IFEval)
   Conclude with the gap: no study unifies all five.
4. *Case Study: MāyāLucIA* — Introduce the project as an instantiation
   of the questions raised in the introduction.
5. *Methods*
   a. Task design (aburaya audit, author-wp)
   b. Substrate selection (13 substrates, 4 lineages)
   c. Tool configuration (surveyor agent: Glob, Read, Grep, TodoWrite)
   d. Evaluation dimensions (6 qualitative axes + metrics)
6. *Results*
   a. Tool-foraging fingerprints (Glob/Read/Grep/Todo distributions)
   b. Survey depth vs specification quality
   c. Convergence/divergence boundary mapping
   d. Convention adherence (WP-refusal, WP number collision)
   e. Reflection quality ranking
   f. Cost-quality relationship
7. *Discussion*
   a. Monoculture blindness as a practical risk
   b. Tool-use as cognitive phenotype
   c. When to mix and when not to
   d. Limitations (single project, single task class, tool-use only)
8. *Conclusions and Future Directions*
   a. Toward a taxonomy of substrate cognitive styles
   b. Adaptive substrate selection
   c. Convention adherence as an evaluation axis
   d. Living survey update plan
9. *References*

* What This Proves

1. Experimental data from a collaborative-intelligence project can
   produce publishable research — the project is not just infrastructure
   but a research instrument.
2. A living survey can track a fast-moving field without becoming stale
   — if the update convention is lightweight enough.
3. The five-thread framework (fingerprinting, tool-use, diversity,
   beyond-accuracy, convention adherence) identifies a genuine gap in the
   literature — no existing work unifies them.
