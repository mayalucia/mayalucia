* Context and Motivation

The aburaya registry has accumulated cross-reference inconsistencies
across three planes: spirit↔guild bindings, spirit↔power bindings, and
the dual-location identity files (=aburaya/spirits/= vs
=.guardian/identity.yaml= inside submodules). WP-0039 and WP-0040
(both =drafted=) address a subset of these gaps but miss several classes
of inconsistency discovered during this survey. This WP consolidates
all known gaps into a single reconciliation spec, superseding WP-0039
and WP-0040 where they overlap and extending coverage to the planes
they do not reach.

The gaps fall into five categories:

1. *Spirit↔Guild binding gaps* — spirits without guild fields, guilds
   listing phantom spirits, system.md listing stale counts.
2. *Power cross-reference gaps* — spirits claiming powers that have no
   =aburaya/powers/<name>.md= on disk; powers on disk claimed by no spirit.
3. *Identity duplication drift* — four spirits have diverged copies
   between =aburaya/spirits/<name>/identity.yaml= and
   =<module>/.guardian/identity.yaml=.
4. *system.md staleness* — guild list, spirit list, directory
   structure, and submodule table omit percept guild, dixa spirit,
   =domains/percept=, and =modules/sphota=.
5. *Prior WP disagreement* — WP-0039 proposes =guild: mayalucia= for
   mayadev/mu2tau; WP-0040 proposes =guild: trans-guild=. Neither is
   tightened. This WP must resolve the question or mark it explicitly
   for human decision.

** Part A: Resolve mayadev and mu2tau guild assignment

/Human decision required./ Two options:

*Option 1: =guild: mayalucia=* — Both serve the organisation as a

mayalucia guild already lists three spirits that serve the whole; two

** Part C: Reconcile identity duplication (=aburaya/spirits/= vs =.guardian/=)

/Human decision required./ The project must designate one canonical
location for identity data.

*Option 1: =aburaya/spirits/= is canonical.* Update the four
=aburaya/spirits/= copies to strip =nature:= blocks and change
=system_prompt= to =system.md=, matching the =.guardian/= versions. The
=.guardian/identity.yaml= in submodules becomes a symlink or generated
copy.

*Option 2: =.guardian/= is canonical.* The guardian's identity lives
with its worksite. =aburaya/spirits/= becomes a derived index. This
means the aburaya registry is no longer the single source of truth.

*Option 3: Keep both, with a documented relationship.* =aburaya/= holds
the master record (including =nature:= with =tbd= for uninstantiated
spirits). =.guardian/= holds the operational subset (no =nature:=, as per
WP-0029). Accept that the two will drift and rely on dixa to detect it.

*Recommendation:* Option 3 is the current de facto state. Option 1 is
cleaner. Defer to human judgment. At minimum, add a comment to each
=.guardian/identity.yaml= noting it is derived from =aburaya/spirits/=
and may diverge. Better: update the four =aburaya/spirits/= files to
match WP-0029 conventions (strip =nature:= block, use =system.md=) and
treat =aburaya/= as canonical.

Edits (if Option 1 chosen):

* What This Proves

The aburaya registry is the project's self-model. When that model
drifts from reality — phantom spirits, missing guild assignments,
diverged copies, stale system.md — spirits that cold-start against it
inherit a distorted picture. This WP proves that the project can
detect drift across all five planes (spirit↔guild, spirit↔power,
identity duplication, system.md, prior-WP conflicts) and specify
repairs precisely enough for autonomous execution.

It also demonstrates a failure mode of organic growth: three
independent WPs (0038, 0039, 0040) attacked overlapping subsets of
the same problem without consolidation. This WP is the consolidation.
