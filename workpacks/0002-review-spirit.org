* Motivation

MāyāLucIA produces a distinctive kind of document we have been calling
"review reports" but which is really a family of related activities:

- /Landscape surveys/ — mapping what exists in a field (GIS MCP servers,
  executable manuscript tools, connectomics papers, AI evaluation frameworks)
- /Position papers/ — placing our own work against the surveyed landscape
  (autonomy agreements vs. CIRL, DMT vs. EleutherAI harness)
- /Strategic briefings/ — synthesising regulatory, market, and technical
  context to argue for a direction (DMT for AI agents)
- /Gap analyses/ — what is missing, what is our opening, what remains unknown
  (bravli landscape "what could we understand next")

These are not independent genres. A single document often passes through
several: the DMT report opens with a gap analysis (the universal validation
problem), surveys existing frameworks (EleutherAI, Inspect AI, Brain-Score),
positions DMT against them, and closes with a strategic plan. The executable
manuscripts survey traces a genealogy (Knuth → Jupyter → Quarto → Org-babel),
assesses the commercial SOTA, and identifies where our practice sits.

The pattern recurs across every domain — bravli, parbati, agency, dmt-eval —
and appears to be a core intellectual practice of the project: /understand the
field by building a map of it, then locate yourself on the map/.

We want to distil this practice into a reproducible skill that an agent can
perform, and commission a spirit to carry it.

* Phase 1: Pattern Extraction

** Objective

Read the corpus above and produce an ORG document that captures the
/practice/ — not the content of any single review, but the patterns that
recur across all of them. This is a second-order analysis: the review
of reviews.

** What to extract

*** 1. Document architecture patterns

How are these documents structured? What sections recur? What is the
typical arc? Identify the recurring skeleton:

- How do they open? (Problem statement? Historical narrative? Gap?)
- How do they survey? (Chronological genealogy? Category matrix? Concentric rings?)
- How do they compare? (Feature tables? Prose contrasts? Quadrant analysis?)
- How do they position? (Gap identification? Competitive advantage? Philosophical grounding?)
- How do they close? (Action items? Open questions? Strategic plan?)

*** 2. Web research patterns

What kinds of external information go into these documents? Characterise
the research practice:

- /Regulatory and standards/: EU AI Act, NIST, ISO — how are these sourced
  and integrated?
- /Academic literature/: papers, citations, intellectual genealogy — how deep
  does the review go? What counts as sufficient coverage?
- /Tools and products/: GitHub repos, PyPI packages, commercial offerings —
  what do we look for? (stars, version, API patterns, limitations)
- /Market and timing/: why-now arguments — how do we construct urgency?

*** 3. Comparison methodology

How does the project compare its own work to what it finds?

- What are the recurring comparison dimensions? (Report generation, adapter
  patterns, extensibility, domain specificity)
- When do we use tables? When prose? When quadrant diagrams?
- How do we handle "closest existing work"? (The autonomy paper's §3 is a
  model: each ancestor gets a paragraph, then a "but" clause.)
- What is our relationship to the things we compare against — competitor?
  successor? orthogonal? How is this framed?

*** 4. Voice and epistemic stance

What is the rhetorical stance of these documents?

- How does uncertainty get expressed? ("immature state", "an open question",
  "whether the market exists is an open question")
- When do we use the first person? When impersonal?
- What is the ratio of reporting to judgment?
- How does domain expertise surface? (The physics training, the BBP years,
  the Indian epistemological tradition)

*** 5. Integration with the project cycle

How do reviews connect to the Measure → Model → Manifest → Evaluate cycle?

- Reviews appear to live in the /Measure/ phase (surveying what exists) and
  the /Evaluate/ phase (positioning against it)
- Do they always precede building? Or do some emerge after?
- How do reviews feed into commissioning decisions? (The GIS review → parbati
  tooling choices; the DMT survey → project charter)

** Deliverable

An ORG file: =develop/review-practice.org= — a pattern language for the
project's review work. This document should be detailed enough that an agent
could, given a domain and a question, produce a review document in the
project's established style.

   will be grounded in the actual practice, not speculated in advance.
