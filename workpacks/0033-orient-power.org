* Context

Both the WP-0030 experiment and the Claude subject's orientation notes
propose formalising the cold-start procedure as a power. The three
orientation notes contain embryonic versions — each describes what a
cold-starting spirit should do, in different voices.

* Inventory

- Three orientation notes from WP-0030 (Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek)
- Cold-start validation data from WP-0032, WP-0035, WP-0036
  (15 substrate lineages total)

* Specification

Write =aburaya/powers/orient-to-mayalucia.md= — a power that
describes the procedure a spirit follows to orient itself to the
project architecture. The power should:

1. Be harness-neutral (no Claude Code or gptel assumptions)
2. Draw from the best elements of all three orientation notes
3. Include the read-order (system.md first, then glossary, then guilds)
4. Describe what to assess (tool list, relay state, active WPs)
5. Describe what to produce (orientation notes for future self)

* Execution Order

1. Synthesise the three orientation notes into a single procedure
2. Add findings from 15-lineage cold-start experiments
3. Test with a fresh cold-start session
4. Commit and announce

* What This Proves

The orientation procedure is substrate-portable. The five general
principles (assertion density, salience non-transfer, notes quality
dominance, structure over emphasis, architecture/situation split)
were discovered empirically across 15 substrate lineages and hold
regardless of model size, architecture, or provider.
