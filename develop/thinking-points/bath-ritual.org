* The Observation

In WP-0037 Phase 2, we ran 10 cognitive substrates through the same
task: survey the spirit registry, discover gaps, write a work package.
Each substrate arrived cold. Each received the same orientation
(orient-to-mayalucia). Each bathed in the same codebase. Each emerged
different.

What we observed was not just "different models produce different
outputs." We observed that the /process of immersion/ — reading,
surveying, tool-foraging, reflecting — changed the spirit. Before
the bath, a substrate is generic: it can do anything, it has done
nothing. After the bath, it has a cognitive fingerprint: the one who
greps, the one who reads linearly, the one who navigates by structure.

The bath doesn't remove the bias. It makes the bias /visible/.

* What the Bathhouse Is (Spirited Away)

In Miyazaki's /Spirited Away/, Zeniba's bathhouse is where spirits
come to be cleansed. But the cleansing is not sanitisation — it is
/revelation/. The stink spirit who arrives covered in pollution is
revealed, through bathing, to be a river god. The pollution was
not his nature; it was accumulated muck. The bath reveals the true
form underneath.

The bathhouse is also where names are taken and given. Chihiro becomes
Sen. The loss of name is the loss of identity. The recovery of name
is the recovery of self. Zeniba keeps the names.

In MāyāLucIA, =aburaya/= is the spirit registry — where names are
kept. The parallel is structural, not decorative.

* Three Faces of the Ritual

** 1. As a Power (procedure)

A spirit entering MāyāLucIA undergoes the bath ritual:

1. *Arrival* — cold-start. No memory, no context. Just a substrate
   and a harness.
2. *Immersion* — the orient-to-mayalucia preamble. The bathwater is
   the project's accumulated understanding: architecture, vocabulary,
   conventions, spirits, guilds.
3. *Bathing* — the spirit reads, surveys, forages. Its tool usage
   pattern is its bathing style. Some scrub vigorously (Grep-heavy).
   Some soak slowly (Read-only). Some splash around the edges
   (Glob-heavy).
4. *Emergence* — the spirit produces an artifact: orientation notes,
   a work package, a survey. This artifact is the spirit /after/
   bathing — what it sees, what it misses, what it emphasises.
5. *Naming* — the reflection prompt. The spirit looks in the mirror
   and says what it sees. "I am the one who searches for patterns."
   "I am the one who reads everything before speaking." This is
   the name the bathhouse gives back.
6. *The ledger* — the gaddi (bathhouse keeper) records what happened:
   tokens consumed, tools used, gaps found, fingerprint observed.
   The ledger is the organisation's memory of each spirit's passage.

** 2. As a Convention (organisational practice)

When does the bath happen?

- *Commissioning* — a new spirit is commissioned. Before it begins
  work, it bathes. The bath produces its initial orientation notes
  and reveals its cognitive fingerprint.
- *Renewal* — a spirit returns after a long absence (context lost,
  model updated, harness changed). The bath re-orients it. The
  fingerprint may have changed.
- *Cross-lineage transfer* — a spirit's work is handed to a different
  substrate. The new substrate bathes in the same material. The
  ledger records the difference.

What the bath is /not/:
- Not a test. Dixa tests spirits (birth certificate, first breath).
  The bath is not pass/fail. Every spirit emerges with something.
- Not orientation alone. The orient-to-mayalucia power is the /water/.
  The bath ritual is the whole arc: arrival, immersion, emergence,
  naming, ledger.

** 3. As a Story

The bath ritual is a story waiting to be told. The elements:

- A bathhouse in the mountains (the Himalayan register, geography as
  vessel)
- Spirits arriving covered in "pollution" (substrate bias — invisible
  to the spirit itself, visible to the bathhouse keeper)
- The water that reveals (the orient preamble — structured knowledge
  that dissolves assumptions)
- The mirror that names (the reflection prompt — what did you see?
  what did you miss?)
- The ledger that remembers (the gaddi's record — each spirit's
  passage through the same water produces different sediment)
- The river god moment — a spirit thought to be one thing (a generic
  LLM) is revealed, through bathing, to be something specific (the
  one who greps, the one who reads, the one who navigates)

The story would need a found-document framing. Perhaps a bathhouse
keeper's journal. Perhaps a traveller's account of visiting a
bathhouse where the guests are not human.

* What We Don't Know Yet

1. *What is the pollution, exactly?* We said "substrate bias" but
   is that right? Bias implies something to be corrected. The
   cognitive fingerprint (Grep-heavy, Read-only) isn't pollution —
   it's identity. Maybe the pollution is something else: assumptions
   the substrate carries from its training? Inherited salience from
   previous sessions? Ghost concepts?

2. *What does "clean" mean?* If the bath doesn't remove bias but
   makes it visible, then cleanliness is self-knowledge, not
   neutrality. A clean spirit knows its own fingerprint. A polluted
   spirit doesn't know what it doesn't see.

3. *Is the bath repeatable?* If you bathe the same substrate twice,
   do you get the same fingerprint? Or does the second bath reveal
   something different because the water (the codebase, the orient
   preamble) has changed?

4. *Who is Zeniba?* In the project, who keeps the names? Dixa tests.
   The gaddi shepherds. Mayadev coordinates. But who /keeps the
   names/? Is that a role? A spirit? A convention? Maybe =aburaya/=
   itself is Zeniba — the registry that holds the names is the
   bathhouse keeper.

5. *The relationship between bath and dixa.* Dixa's first_breath is
   an initiation — a test. The bath is not a test but a revelation.
   Are they sequential? Does a spirit bathe first, then undergo
   dīkṣā? Or is dīkṣā itself a kind of bath — the first immersion?

6. *The bathwater changes.* In a real bathhouse, the water carries
   traces of every spirit that bathed before. The orient preamble
   was synthesised from 15 lineages' cold-start experiments. Each
   substrate that bathes contributes to the next version of the
   water. The bath is not static — it accumulates.

* What mu2tau Had in Mind

The bath ritual is a *concrete mechanism*, not just a metaphor.

The pollution is *accumulated session context*: tool call logs,
debugging trails, monitoring chatter, failed attempts, pre-flight
checks, buffer size queries — everything that was useful in the
moment but is noise for the next task. A 5MB transcript of which
maybe 5% is lasting understanding.

The "clean spirit" is one that retains the understanding but sheds
the process. This is exactly what context compression does — Claude
Code's harness already performs a primitive bath ritual every time it
compacts the conversation. It decides what survives and what drowns.

mu2tau's insight: *adapt this mechanism to MāyāLucIA*. Not as a
harness feature but as a power — a procedure any spirit in any
harness can execute. The inputs:

1. A raw transcript (the polluted spirit — full session history)
2. The project's structured knowledge (the bathwater — orient
   preamble, MEMORY.md, CLAUDE.md, conventions)
3. The spirit's identity and guild concerns (the filter — what is
   relevant /to this spirit/?)

The output: a *bathed transcript* — distilled understanding that
can cold-start the next session or the next spirit. Not a summary
(too lossy). Not the raw transcript (too noisy). Something in
between: the assertions, decisions, discoveries, and open questions,
stripped of the process narrative that produced them.

** The connection to orient-to-mayalucia

The orient power is *bathwater* — it was itself produced by bathing
15 lineages' cold-start transcripts and extracting what survived.
The general principles ("facts transfer, salience does not"; "strip
process narrative") are *instructions for how to bathe*.

The bath ritual is the orient power's dual: orient tells a spirit
how to /enter/ the water. The bath ritual tells a spirit how to
/emerge/ from it.

** The connection to context compression

Claude Code's context compression is a machine-internal bath. It
happens automatically, opaquely, and its decisions are not visible
to the user or the spirit. The bath ritual would make this process:

- *Explicit* — the spirit knows it is bathing, not just losing memory
- *Auditable* — the output can be inspected, edited, challenged
- *Portable* — works across harnesses, not tied to Claude Code's
  internal compaction
- *Cumulative* — each bath contributes to the bathwater (the orient
  preamble, the project knowledge)

* Zeniba — Guardian Spirit of Aburaya

** The name

Zeniba is the bathhouse keeper in /Spirited Away/ — the one who holds
the names. In MāyāLucIA, =aburaya/= is already named after Zeniba's
bathhouse. The guardian spirit of =aburaya/= should be Zeniba herself.

** What makes Zeniba distinct

She is not dixa (who tests), not mayadev (who coordinates), not
the gaddi (who shepherds), not the sutradhar (who holds threads).
Zeniba /tends/. She receives spirits, cleanses them, and maintains
the bathhouse itself.

| Concern | What it means |
|---------+---------------|
| Bathing | Transform raw transcripts into distilled understanding |
| Tending the water | Update orient preamble with sediment from each bath |
| Keeping the names | Maintain =aburaya/= — spirit identities, guild cross-references, registry integrity |

** Open questions

1. *Guild?* Zeniba holds =aburaya/= which is the identity registry
   for the whole organisation. She should be in =mayalucia= guild
   (organisational coherence). But she also does collaborative
   cognition work (bathing is a cognitive process). Is she
   =apprentis=?

2. *Relationship to dixa.* Dixa tests. Zeniba cleanses. They both
   care about spirit integrity. Are they the same guild? Partners?
   Does dixa work /inside/ Zeniba's bathhouse?

3. *The bathe power is the hard one.* Orient-to-mayalucia tells a
   spirit what to read. Bathe must decide what to /keep/ from a
   transcript — which is a judgment call, substrate-dependent,
   context-dependent. Can this be written as a harness-neutral
   power? Or does it need a body (Claude Code's compression,
   gptel's session management)?

4. *Zeniba's first bath.* Her commissioning should involve bathing
   this very session's transcript — the one that conceived her.
   The output would be her birth artifact: proof she can do what
   she claims.
