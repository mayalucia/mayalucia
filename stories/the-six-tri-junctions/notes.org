#+title: Notes — The Six Tri-Junctions
#+date: 2026-03-09

* Origin

Experiment 02 (Substrate Diversity, WP-0030) tested three AI
lineages — Claude Opus 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro, DeepSeek V3.2-Speciale
— on the same architecture documents. The finding: no single lineage
sees the full picture. Each sees what the others cannot. The union of
perspectives is richer than any individual view.

"The Three Readers" (story #15) translated this finding into the
Tirthan rest house setting — Mehra's three student drawings. mu2tau
proposed expanding the geographic range: "we can come up with a few
more metaphors... we will change the geography, staying within western
himalaya." He identified tri-junctions as the structural metaphor —
places where three valleys, traditions, or languages converge.

Three parallel research agents surveyed the Parvati headwall, the
trans-border Karakoram, and the Nanga Parbat massif. Six geographic
options emerged. mu2tau's response: "why not write all of these, as
six different chapters of a long story."

* Story Plan

** Structure

Six self-contained chapters, each at a different tri-junction. The
Thread Walker moves from familiar territory (upper Parvati) north
and west into progressively less-visited country, ending in the
Karakoram. Prefatory note + six chapters + coda.

** The itinerary

This is the first story structured as an itinerary — movement across
geography rather than encounter at a single place. The movement itself
carries meaning: each step north takes the Thread Walker further from
known country, and the metaphors shift accordingly (from weather → to
tradition → to language → to spirit).

* Technical Context

The substrate diversity experiment revealed three classes of finding:

- Line-by-line bias detection (Claude → the measurer, six tributaries)
- Structural bias detection (Gemini → the climber, ironic gap)
- Categorical bias detection (DeepSeek → the reader, seven assumptions)

"The Three Readers" translates these at close range (one slab, three
drawings). "The Six Tri-Junctions" translates the *principle* at large
range — the same insight refracted through six geographies.
