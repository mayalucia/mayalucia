#+author: mu2tau + mayadev
#+date: 2026-03-12

* Origin

Experiment 03 — Procedural Realism (2026-03-11). The experiment
used Stable Diffusion 1.5 to generate reference images of the
Indus gorge at Thalpan, then wrote procedural code driven by SRTM
elevation data to approximate those images algorithmically. The
discovery: the SRTM data — ninety-metre grid, one-metre vertical
resolution — produced a more correct image than the generative
model, because it contained the actual asymmetry of the gorge
(Kohistan arc wall to 4,300m in 5km ENE vs Indian plate wall to
1,800m WSW), while the generative model produced a symmetrical
"average gorge" that looked like no place in particular.

The pipeline inverted: the model was supposed to be the reference;
instead, the measurement became the authority and the model became
the surface decorator.

* Setting

Same as The Peg-Path (#18) and The Seven Readers (#20): the
Thalpan petroglyph terrace, upper Indus gorge, Diamer district.
The Thread Walker is still here — this story takes place
during or after those days on the terrace.

* Core Tension

The relationship between measurement and imagination. The contour
(measured shape) vs the hachure (drawn impression). The fact that
a satellite's crude numbers can produce the gorge more truly than
a painter's skilled imagination.

Secondary tension: the petroglyphs are ten thousand years of
human seeing; the survey is a machine's blind measurement. Both
produce records. Neither contains the ibex.

* Document Type

Field notes — the Thread Walker meets a woman who makes pictures
from measurements.

* Voice References

The Peg-Path (#18), The Seven Readers (#20), The Mineral
Deposits (#9). Same geography, same register. The survey-report
precision of #9 is especially relevant — this story deals with
measurement as a mode of knowing.
