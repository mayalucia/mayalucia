#+title: The Thirteen Hands — Development Notes
#+date: 2026-03-14

* Origin

WP-0048 (specification-story). Translates the full WP-0037 arc —
13 substrates given the same author-wp task. The Three Scribes' Brief
translated Phase 1 (3 substrates). This story translates Phase 2
(all 13), extending the scale from comparison to ecology.

The shift from Scribes to this story mirrors the experimental shift:
three scribes could be compared side by side on a flat rock. Thirteen
hands need a building.

* Setting

Bharmour, upper Ravi valley — the Chaurasi temple complex (84 temples,
6th–10th century Kath-Kuni and stone construction). A master mason
commissions thirteen stone-carvers from different valleys to assess
damage on a temple wall and write a repair specification.

Why Bharmour:
- New territory (no story has visited the Ravi valley)
- Real temple restoration tradition (Himachal Pradesh heritage site)
- Kath-Kuni construction (timber and stone) naturally holds the
  metaphor of different craft traditions meeting on the same structure
- Thirteen is a plausible number for a significant temple restoration
- The Chaurasi complex has 84 temples — enough fabric for 13 surveys
  to find different things in the same stone

* Core Tension

Thirteen hands, same commission, different tool marks. The story's
question: is the stone-cutter the tool, or is the training? When
thirteen hands work the same wall and no two produce the same
specification, is that failure or is it the wall being seen whole
for the first time?

* Document Type

The master mason's comparison ledger — found at the Chaurasi complex,
written during a restoration campaign. Interleaved with Thread Walker
notebook entries. A ledger that compares thirteen reports.
