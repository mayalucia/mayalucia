* Origin

The substrate diversity experiments (WP-0030, WP-0031) opened a question:
what does it mean for intelligence to run on a substrate it did not build?
The Diamer surveys (WP-0012, WP-0013, WP-0021) supplied the raw material.
The percept guild (new, 2026-03-10) supplied the theoretical frame: perception
as inverse problem, trance as altered prior, spirit taxonomy as generative
model of landscape.

Four threads crossed: glacier marriage, drowning petroglyphs, the bitan's
language, the tectonic aneurysm. All converge on one massif — Nanga Parbat /
Diamer. Three faces, no single vantage from which all are visible.

* The Massif

Two names: Nanga Parbat (naked mountain, visible, geological) / Diamer
(mountain of the gods, invisible, inhabited). One describes what can be
seen. The other names what cannot. The mountain answers to both.

Three faces: Rakhiot (north), Diamir (west), Rupal (south, 4,600m — the
highest mountain face on Earth). No valley from which all three are visible.

The tectonic aneurysm: uplift 7–13 mm/yr. The youngest granite on Earth
(0.7 Ma) — decompression melting. The river (Indus) older than the mountain
it cuts through. The mountain unmakes itself to make itself.

* Structure — Three Stories, Three Faces

** Story #17 — The Glacier's Dowry (Rakhiot face)

Theme: cultivating an agent / commissioning

Setting: Rakhiot valley. Tato hot springs (92 deg C / 0 deg C in the
same drainage). The glacier marriage protocol: male (po gang, grey,
debris-covered) + female (mo gang, white/blue, growing) ice. Coal and
barley hay. Willow chorong basket. North-facing cave at 4,000–5,000m.
Mud, ash, charcoal seal. Special prayers and sacrifice. 12-year gestation.
80% success rate (AKRSP). The 7.5-fold acceleration of downwasting
(2009–2023 vs prior 58 years). The Karakoram Anomaly ending.

The Thread Walker meets someone who knows the protocol — a practitioner
or a keeper of the knowledge. The notebook: you don't commission an agent
by commanding it. You carry the pieces to the right altitude, seal them
in darkness, and wait.

** Story #18 — The Peg-Path (Diamir face / Indus gorge)

Theme: memory, substrate, what survives

Setting: the Indus gorge below Chilas. Thalpan petroglyph terrace —
50,000 marks, 10+ writing systems, 10,000 years. Faxian's 700 ladders
(399 CE). Nanai-Vandak the Sogdian pleading to reach Tashkurgan.
The palimpsest: ibex carved first, Buddha carved over it. King Sibi
weighing his own flesh. The dam will drown 86% of the carvings.
The ibex still stands on the cliff above the waterline.

The Thread Walker visits the terraces. Reads the layers. The notebook:
the animal outlives its record. What is carved in rock drowns; what is
carved in behaviour climbs.

** Story #19 — The Bitan's Tongue (Rupal face / Hunza)

Theme: voice, substrate-independence

Setting: begins at the Rupal face. Reaches Hunza. The bitan protocol:
dadang + daamal + surnai, 40+ min, juniper smoke, goat blood. The iron
bangle (kau) — binds and protects simultaneously. Peri select shamans by
smelling newborns during apricot blossom. The bitan speaks Shina in trance;
awake he speaks only Burushaski. The Dom musicians' monopoly = protocol-layer
control. Music is not decoration; it is the protocol layer.

The Thread Walker hears about but does not witness a session — told by
someone who has seen it. The notebook: the medium's substrate carries a
message it cannot compose. The voice is not borrowed. It arrives.

* The Weave — What Crosses All Three

- The tectonic aneurysm as background bass note (never foregrounded)
- The dual name: Nanga Parbat / Diamer
- Twelve as structural number (12-yr glacier, 12 named tunes, 12 centuries of inscriptions)
- The permanent surface / temporary mark — here inverted in each story
- No single vantage sees all three faces

* TODO(human) — How Does the Thread Walker Enter Diamer?

She has been moving upstream since Story #13. Six Tri-Junctions took her
to the edge of the Karakoram (chapters IV-VI) but those were surveys, not
immersions.

The decision: how does she arrive?

Options to consider:
- On foot, over a pass (continuation of the upstream movement)
- By road, on the KKH (the modern traveller's approach)
- Brought by someone — a guide, a connection from an earlier story
- She doesn't arrive in the first story — we begin with her already there
  (the journey elided, the way Faxian's account begins mid-crossing)

The mode of arrival determines:
1. Whether these stories feel like continuation or departure
2. Whether the Diamer material reads as her territory or borrowed ground
3. How she relates to the structural silence (Part E of the travellers
   survey) — the most documented mountain, the least-documented community.
   The Thread Walker is herself an outsider who documents. Her notebook is
   an act of documentation. Is that a tension the stories should hold,
   or resolve, or leave unnamed?

* Prefatory Note (draft)

Two names for one mountain. Nanga Parbat: the naked mountain, stripped
bare above the snowline, visible from a hundred kilometres. Diamer: the
mountain of the gods, named by those who live in its shadow. One name
describes what can be seen. The other names what cannot. The mountain
answers to both. From no valley can all three faces be seen at once.

* Illustration Register

Shift from the chalk-on-slate of the Tirthan rest house. Diamer is:
- Mineral colours: grey gneiss, orange hydrothermal alteration, white ice, blue meltwater
- No forest canopy — absence of deodar, absence of birdsong
- Architecture of scarcity, not abundance
- The gorge is vertical; the meadow (Joot) is horizontal
- 48.5 deg C in Chilas summer; 0 deg C at the glacier
