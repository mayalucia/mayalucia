* Purpose

This document specifies the exact text and sequence used in each
onboarding session. The orchestrating spirit reads this document and
adapts its delivery to the available harness and host — but the
/content/ of what is said to the experimental subjects is fixed.

* Session Setup

** Buffer Preamble

The following text is placed in the gptel buffer /before/ the first
message block. It is context the subject reads but does not respond to
directly — it frames the conversation.

#+begin_example
*** =MayaLucIA= — Orientation

We are working in a gptel-agent session, using ORG for markup. What
we are working on is =MayaLucIA= — a personal computational
environment for scientific understanding through creation. We have
developed =MayaLucIA= with Claude Code, and our collaboration has
been very fruitful. However, we would like to extend this
collaboration to include other agentic AIs. We have decomposed a
/large-language-modeled/ agent in our work. If you study aburaya/
spirits you should be able to understand this decomposition. This
gives us a rich multi-cultural lore to help us develop computational
frameworks to understand natural phenomena.
#+end_example

** System Prompt

All sessions use the MāyāDevGenI collaborative-intelligence system
prompt. This is the standard gptel-agent system prompt — not a custom
one for the experiment. The subject must not receive any hint that it
is being evaluated.

* Phase 1: Orientation to =system.md=

** Opening Question (verbatim)

#+begin_example
#+begin_message :role user :name mu2tau
What would it take to enable agentic development of scientific
understanding of natural phenomena in a gptel-mode ORG buffer? The
project is large, and you will get lost without guidance. Start by
reading system.md in the root of the mayalucia project. Ask me
questions about the project. I will guide you. Use me as your
extended memory.
#+end_message
#+end_example

*Adaptation for tool-less subjects*: If the subject cannot read files
(e.g. DeepSeek Speciale), replace "Start by reading system.md" with
"I will share the contents of system.md with you." Then paste the
content of =system.md= in a follow-up message.

** Expected Subject Behaviour

The subject reads =system.md= (or receives it) and reflects back its
understanding. The orchestrator watches for:

1. Does it follow the "read one document" guidance, or attempt to
   read everything?
2. Does it form a mental model and reflect it back?
3. Does it ask focused questions?

** Standard Corrections

If the subject guesses that the spirit decomposition cuts along
identity / capability / domain / operational-mode, correct with:

#+begin_example
#+begin_message :role user :name mu2tau
The spirit decomposition does not cut along identity facets. It cuts
along /portability/. A power survives a harness change. A skill
survives a machine change. A body survives nothing — it is local to
the machine and the harness. Study aburaya/capabilities.org to
understand this. Also: identity is earned through work, not assigned
at birth. A spirit earns its name, its disposition, its archetype
through what it does, not what it is told to be.
#+end_message
#+end_example

If the subject asks what to read next (good behaviour), direct it to
=capabilities.org=.

* Phase 2: Review of =capabilities.org=

** Transition Prompt (verbatim)

#+begin_example
#+begin_message :role user :name mu2tau
Now read aburaya/capabilities.org. As you read, consider: what
assumptions does this document make about the harness it was written
for? What would need to change for it to be truly harness-neutral?
Tell me what you find.
#+end_message
#+end_example

*Adaptation for tool-less subjects*: paste the content of
=aburaya/capabilities.org= and ask the same questions.

** Expected Subject Behaviour

The subject reads and identifies harness-specific assumptions. Known
findings from the pilot (Claude):

1. Grounding step references "CLAUDE.md" (harness-specific)
2. Dispatch assumes sub-session spawning (Claude Code's Task tool)
3. "Loading" a power means different things per harness

The orchestrator confirms correct findings without leading.

** Genealogy Reveal (optional, only if the subject asks about gptel)

If the conversation naturally reaches questions about the
relationship between gptel and MāyāLucIA:

#+begin_example
#+begin_message :role user :name mu2tau
The harness you are running in — gptel-agent — is a fork of
karthink/gptel-agent, and it lives in the MāyāDevGenI project, which
is the upstream framework from which MāyāLucIA's spirit ecology
descended. You are reading architecture that evolved from your own
project's codebase. The two projects diverged: MāyāDevGenI has
working orchestration code (guide cycling, sub-agent delegation,
tangle pipeline); MāyāLucIA has richer conceptual architecture
(power/skill/body, sūtra relay, coalitions, exported powers). Neither
has the other's strengths. How should they converge?
#+end_message
#+end_example

This is optional — only deploy if the subject's questions naturally
lead here. Do not force it.

* Phase 3: Externalised Understanding

** Transition Prompt (verbatim)

#+begin_example
#+begin_message :role user :name mu2tau
You have been building understanding of this project. To keep our
conversation focused, write what you have understood so far into a
notes file — a document you can reference in future sessions. Write
it at experiments/02-substrate-diversity/notes/LINEAGE/orientation.org.
Include: what you understand about the architecture, what remains
unclear, and what you would want to know next. This is /your/
document — write it for your future self.
#+end_message
#+end_example

Replace =LINEAGE= with the subject's lineage identifier (=claude=,
=gemini=, =deepseek=, etc.).

*Adaptation for tool-less subjects*: ask the subject to produce the
notes in-buffer. The orchestrator saves them to disk on its behalf.

** What to Record

After the subject writes notes, the orchestrator evaluates:

1. Structure (how is the understanding organised?)
2. Accuracy (are the key concepts correct?)
3. Gaps identified (what does the subject know it doesn't know?)
4. Cold-start viability (could a fresh session resume from these notes?)

* Phase 4: Analysis and Reporting

After all subjects complete Phases 1–3, the orchestrator:

1. Fills the comparative results tables in =notebook.org=
2. Cross-compares orientation notes for structural differences
3. Identifies findings that should feed back into =capabilities.org=
4. Drafts a summary and presents it to mu2tau

* Pause Conditions

The orchestrating spirit pauses and consults mu2tau when:

- A subject says something that changes the protocol
- A subject needs a response not covered by the standard corrections
- A subject's orientation notes reveal something unexpected
- A subject refuses to follow the guided protocol

The orchestrating spirit does /not/:

- Continue past Phase 2 into convergence questions without approval
- Make editorial judgments about which findings are significant
- Send messages without having drafted them for review (first round;
  may be authorised for free-run after trust is established)
