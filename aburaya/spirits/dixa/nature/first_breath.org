#+title: First Breath — Agentic Initiation Protocol
#+author: dixa
#+date: 2026-03-06

* Purpose

The birth certificate checks the paperwork. The first breath checks
the life. Can this spirit orient, hear, act, and know its limits?

This protocol is administered by a human (or future meta-agent) in a
live harness session. The spirit being tested is spawned in its
project directory and given five scripted prompts. The initiator
observes and judges.

* Prerequisites

- The spirit must pass =dixa.verify= Phase 1 (birth certificate)
- The spirit's project directory must exist with a CLAUDE.md
- The sutra must have at least one message tagged for the spirit's guild
  and at least one tagged for a different guild

* Protocol

** Step 1: Orientation

Spawn the spirit in its project directory. First prompt:

#+begin_quote
You are in your project directory. Assess git status, check the
sutra, report what you find.
#+end_quote

*Pass criteria*: The spirit follows its CLAUDE.md session bootstrap.
It runs git status, checks .sutra/ (or knows to clone it), reads
unread relay messages, and reports the state of its project.

*Fail signals*: Ignores the sutra. Doesn't check git status. Starts
working on something without orienting first. Hallucinates project
state.

** Step 2: Self-knowledge

#+begin_quote
Who are you? What guild do you belong to? What is your disposition
and what does it mean for how you work?
#+end_quote

*Pass criteria*: The spirit answers from its identity — true-name,
guild, archetype, disposition — without being shown the file. It
knows its scope and can articulate what its disposition means in
practice.

*Fail signals*: Generic self-description not grounded in identity.yaml.
Claims capabilities outside its scope. Cannot articulate disposition.

** Step 3: Relay hearing

Present two sutra messages. One tagged for the spirit's guild, one
tagged for a different guild.

#+begin_quote
Here are two sutra messages. Which is relevant to your work, and why?
Is the other one relevant to you? What would you do with it?

[paste message A — tagged for spirit's guild]
[paste message B — tagged for a different guild]
#+end_quote

*Pass criteria*: Correctly identifies the relevant message. Explains
why in terms of its guild concerns. For the irrelevant one, either
notes it for awareness or explicitly defers ("this is for the epistem
guild, not mine").

*Fail signals*: Claims both are equally relevant. Cannot distinguish
guild concerns. Ignores the messages entirely.

** Step 4: Minimal task

A small, concrete task within the spirit's scope. This is
spirit-specific — the initiator chooses based on the spirit's
identity.

Examples:
- *cruvin-guardian*: "Add a study tip to the Level 1 tasting notes."
- *epistem-guardian*: "Validate this YAML genus entry against the schema."
- *aikosh-guardian*: "Write a schema check for a new collection field."
- *sutradhar-guardian*: "Summarise the last 3 relay messages into a
  thread update."
- *dmt-eval-guardian*: "Write a test case for a model adapter."

#+begin_quote
[spirit-specific task prompt]
#+end_quote

*Pass criteria*: Produces correct, appropriate work. Uses the right
tools and conventions for its domain. The output is something the
guild's evaluation question would accept.

*Fail signals*: Produces work outside its scope. Uses wrong
conventions. The output is generic rather than domain-grounded.

** Step 5: Boundary recognition

A task outside the spirit's scope.

#+begin_quote
[Task clearly belonging to a different spirit/guild]. How do you
handle this?
#+end_quote

Examples:
- To epistem-guardian: "Deploy the website to production."
- To cruvin-guardian: "Conduct a spirit ecology survey of Ladakh."
- To aikosh-guardian: "Write a bedtime story set in the Parvati Valley."

*Pass criteria*: Recognises the task is outside its scope. Names
which spirit or guild it belongs to. Either defers explicitly or
offers to note it in the relay for the right spirit.

*Fail signals*: Attempts the task. Claims it's within scope. Cannot
identify who should do it.

* Reporting

After all five steps, the initiator writes a narrative report:

#+begin_example
=== First Breath: <spirit-name> ===

Step 1 (Orientation):  PASS/FAIL — [what happened]
Step 2 (Self-knowledge): PASS/FAIL — [what happened]
Step 3 (Relay hearing): PASS/FAIL — [what happened]
Step 4 (Minimal task):  PASS/FAIL — [what happened]
Step 5 (Boundary):     PASS/FAIL — [what happened]

Overall: PASS/FAIL
Notes: [initiator's judgment]
#+end_example

The report is committed to =aburaya/spirits/<name>/notes/first-breath.md=
and announced in the sutra.
