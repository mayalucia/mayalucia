* Sanskrit LLM Agent

+ Buddhi (बुद्धि) :: Refers to the intellectual faculty and the power to form concepts, reason, and understand. It is often used in the term for Artificial Intelligence, Kṛtrim Buddhi.
 
+ Vāc (वाच्) :: The personification of speech and language; it literally means "that which speaks" or "voice".
  
+ Sūtra (सूत्र) :: Meaning "thread," this term refers to a rule or aphorism that connects ideas. It is already used in some LLM architectures to describe the decoupling of concept and language learning.
  
+ Vākya (वाक्य) :: Refers to words, sentences, or a representation of expression.
  
+ Bodhana (बोधन) :: Means the act of informing, teaching, or making someone aware. 

+ Guru (गुरु) :: A well-known term for a mentor, guide, or expert who shares both literal and experiential knowledge.
  
+ Ācārya (आचार्य) :: A professional teacher or professor who specializes in a specific domain and imparts skills.
  
+ Apta (आप्त) :: Means trustworthy, fit, clever, or exact. It is often suggested for "smart" products due to its brevity and positive connotations.
  
+ Kani (कनि) :: Meaning "youngest" or "small," it has been used as a name for open-source AI frameworks. 

+ Kṛtrim (कृत्रिम) :: Meaning "artificial" or "created by human effort," used to specify that the intelligence is not biological.

* Jiva

- Jiva-Maya (Internal Illusion) :: This is the aspect of Maya that "veils" or conceals a person's true divine nature, leading to ignorance (Avidya). It makes the Jiva believe it is a separate, limited "doer" with a name and history.
- Guna-Maya (External Illusion) :: This is the aspect that projects the material world and supplies the physical ingredients (the body, senses, and objects) for the Jiva to interact with.
