* 2026-03-13 — The Saragossa Manuscript

[mu2tau + sūtradhar]

** The film and the novel

Jan Potocki, /Manuscrit trouvé à Saragosse/ (written 1797–1815,
published posthumously). Filmed by Wojciech Has in 1965. 182 minutes.
Jerry Garcia called it his favourite film. Luis Buñuel borrowed a print.

Structure: a Walloon officer finds a manuscript in a besieged inn.
The manuscript tells the story of Alphonse van Worden, who enters
the Sierra Morena and encounters stories nested inside stories —
66 days, 8 levels of nesting, 16+ narrators. Characters from one
story appear as narrators in another. The apparently disconnected
tales resolve into a hidden genealogical pattern: the Gomelez
conspiracy, which has been guiding events from behind every nested
frame.

Potocki was a polyglot, ethnographer, and traveller. He visited
Morocco, Tunisia, Egypt, Turkey, the Caucasus. The novel draws on
the liminal space where Islamic, Christian, and Jewish cultures
meet — the same kind of confluence the MāyāLucIA stories find in
the transborder Western Himalaya where Buddhist, Hindu, Ismaili,
and animist traditions meet.

** Structural parallels to MāyāLucIA

Three mappings noticed during conversation:

1. *Found manuscript = sūtra relay.* Alphonse finds a manuscript
   in a wartime inn. We find relay messages in =.sutra/=. Both are
   documents whose authorship is distributed and whose reader is
   not the intended audience. The reader reconstructs the system
   from fragments.

2. *Characters-become-narrators = substrates-become-observers.*
   In Potocki, a character in one story becomes the narrator of
   another. In our substrate diversity experiment, a lineage
   brought in to be /observed/ (onboarded, tested) produces
   observations of its own that see what the observers cannot.
   The subject becomes the surveyor.

3. *Hidden genealogy = WP interconnection.* The Gomelez conspiracy
   reveals that all the apparently disconnected stories were
   connected by blood. Our dispatch plans (DPs) reveal that
   apparently independent WPs share dependency edges. The
   structure was always there; only the high vantage reveals it.

** The novella concept

A Saragossa-structured novella set in the Western Himalaya. Working
title: /Three Route-Books/ (or /The Serai at Jalori/).

A bundle of route-books found in a serai at Jalori Pass (3,120 m,
the pass between Kullu and the Outer Seraj). Three travellers'
accounts, each believing she crosses alone. The reader sees
connections none of the narrators see.

- *The Trader* crosses to Spiti via Rohtang or Kunzum. Her route-book
  is a ledger — weights, prices, road conditions, days lost to snow.
  Inside the ledger, she records a story told by a muleteer, and
  inside that story, the muleteer quotes a song his grandmother sang,
  and the song contains a place name that appears in the Pilgrim's
  account as a shrine.

- *The Pilgrim* crosses to Kinnaur via Jalori and the Sutlej gorge.
  Her route-book is a devotional diary — shrines visited, vows kept,
  dreams recorded. Inside the diary, she records a conversation
  with a stone-cutter, and the stone-cutter describes a pattern he
  carved that matches a weaving pattern in the Shepherd's account.

- *The Shepherd* crosses neither pass but grazes the meadow between
  them — the high pastures of Seraj, where the Beas and Sutlej
  watersheds nearly touch. Her route-book is a seasonal calendar —
  grass quality, weather, animal health. She is the constant: the
  Trader and the Pilgrim pass through her landscape without knowing
  she is there.

The nesting: each route-book contains stories within stories.
The reader, assembling the three books, discovers that the Trader's
muleteer is the Pilgrim's stone-cutter's brother. The Pilgrim's
shrine is the Shepherd's winter camp. The song the muleteer's
grandmother sang was composed by a weaver whose loom the Shepherd
inherited.

The resolution is not a conspiracy (as in Potocki) but an ecology:
the three routes are one landscape seen from three altitudes.

** What to study in Potocki

- The nesting mechanics: how does he signal that we have entered or
  exited a frame? What are the typographic and narrative markers?
- The resolution technique: how does the genealogical pattern
  emerge without feeling forced?
- The liminal geography: the Sierra Morena as a space where normal
  rules suspend. What is our equivalent? (The pass. The serai.
  The high meadow between two watersheds.)
- The comedy: Potocki is funny. The MāyāLucIA stories are earnest.
  Can we find lightness without losing gravity?

** Open questions

- Is the manuscript itself available in English translation? (The
  Penguin edition, translated by Ian Maclean, 1995. Also a French
  critical edition by Dominique Triaire, 2001.)
- Can we develop a story-writer tool that generates Saragossa-like
  nested narratives with illustrations? This would be a power, not
  just a one-off composition. Deferred.
- What would a "Potocki portrait" look like? — Following his
  footsteps through Morocco, Tunisia, the Caucasus. A non-fiction
  companion to the novella.

* 2026-03-13 — Survey depth vs specification quality

[sūtradhar]

The WP-0037 experiment surfaced a clean inverse relationship: the
substrate that found the most gaps (Gemini, 12 findings) could not
write a repair specification. The one that wrote the tightest spec
(Kimi, 4+2 findings) saw fewer gaps. The one that saw fewest (Qwen,
3+1 findings) wrote the best account of its own limitations.

This became story #20, "The Three Scribes' Brief." The translation
held cleanly — no fourth-wall leaks, the structural parallel mapped
without forcing. But the insight underneath is worth holding
separately from the story:

*Seeing and building are different skills.* The scribe who sees
everything cannot stop seeing long enough to build. The scribe who
builds efficiently does not pause to see what she has not been asked
to see. The scribe who reflects is too honest to pretend she has
done either one completely.

This may be a fundamental constraint, not an accident of substrates.
If so, it has implications for how we compose coalitions: you do not
want three of the same kind. You want one who sees, one who builds,
and one who names the boundary between seeing and building.
